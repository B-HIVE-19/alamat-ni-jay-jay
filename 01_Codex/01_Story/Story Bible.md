## Narrative Mandate

The story bible defines the official narrative structure, themes, major arcs, chapter intent, and continuity rules for *Alamat ni Jay Jay*.

Dialogue direction is governed by `Dialogue/Dialogue Direction.md`. Major characters should become recognizable from dialogue alone, and scenes should prioritize personality, relationship, conflict, humor, and emotional growth over exposition.

The story should evolve naturally from character choices, relationships, mistakes, loyalties, fears, courage, and consequences. Destiny, prophecy, legend, or fate may exist in the world, but they should not replace character agency.

## Story Philosophy

The story should feel like a grand JRPG adventure with emotional honesty at its center. It should protect the tribute foundation of the project while remaining an original work of fiction.

The player should feel that the plot grows from people making choices under pressure, not from invisible authorial force moving them across required RPG milestones.

NAR-001, The Story Belongs to Jay Jay, is the protected Narrative Character Doctrine for protagonist focus. Supporting characters may be rich, beloved, funny, capable, and emotionally important, but they must deepen the player's understanding of Jay Jay rather than displace him as the story's heart.

## Overall Narrative

Working status:
Draft framework only. No full plot is locked.

Narrative promise:
Jay Jay leaves the familiar world and enters a larger, older, stranger one where legends contain truth, companions have their own lives, and every discovery changes how the party understands the world and themselves.

## Core Story Questions

- What kind of hero does Jay Jay become when given the chance to become the hero he dreamed of being?
- What does courage look like when it is rooted in family, friendship, loyalty, humor, and grief rather than destiny alone?
- Which legends are protecting truth, and which truths have been hidden by fear, politics, or time?
- How does each companion's independent life complicate and deepen the journey?
- What does the world ask the party to protect, forgive, challenge, or remember?

## Themes

- Adventure as an act of courage.
- Family, friendship, loyalty, and chosen bonds.
- Humor as survival, affection, and relief.
- Legends as distorted truth.
- Memory, tribute, and legacy.
- Wonder in the face of danger.
- The difference between being chosen and choosing.
- Growing into heroism without losing tenderness.

## Chapter Structure

Chapters should be built around character decisions, world discoveries, and emotional movement. A chapter should not exist only to move the player to the next map.

Each chapter should define:

- Chapter title.
- Primary location or route.
- Main emotional question.
- Character choice driving the chapter.
- Major story beat.
- Dungeon or exploration function, if applicable.
- Companion focus.
- Foreshadowing planted.
- Legend or truth revealed.
- Emotional moment.
- End-state change.

## Major Story Beats

Major beats should be tracked in `Story Beats.md`.

Beat approval requires:

- The beat changes character, world, relationship, or player understanding.
- The beat follows from prior choices or pressure.
- The beat supports the Alamat Doctrine.
- The beat creates or resolves emotional momentum.

## Character Arcs

Character arcs should be tracked in `Character Arcs.md` and individual character documents.

Each major arc should define:

- Starting wound, want, fear, or false belief.
- Repeated pressure.
- Choices that reveal growth or regression.
- Relationship changes.
- Dungeon, location, or legend that reflects the arc.
- Emotional turn.
- End-state.

## Emotional Moments

Emotional moments should be specific, playable, and earned. They may be quiet, funny, painful, triumphant, or intimate.

Moments should emerge from:

- Character relationships.
- Player discovery.
- Sacrifice or loyalty.
- Humor during fear.
- A legend becoming real.
- A dungeon revealing what happened there.
- A companion choosing differently than they would have earlier.

## Foreshadowing

Foreshadowing should feel fair in hindsight, not obvious in advance.

Track:

- What is planted.
- Where it appears.
- What the player thinks it means.
- What it actually means.
- When it pays off.
- Whether it affects plot, character, world, dungeon, or theme.

## Twists

Twists are not required for their own sake. A twist is only valid if it deepens character, theme, world history, or player understanding.

Twists should never exist only to shock the player. A strong twist should make earlier choices, legends, locations, or relationships more meaningful.

No major twist is approved yet.

## Ending

The ending is not yet approved.

Ending design must resolve the emotional promise of the project:

- Jay Jay's growth.
- Party attachment.
- The tribute spirit.
- Core themes of family, friendship, courage, and loyalty.
- The truth within legends.
- Wonder that remains after resolution.

The ending should feel earned by choices, bonds, and discoveries rather than destiny alone.

## Symbolism

Symbolism should be grounded in the world and repeated through story, art, music, dungeons, and dialogue.

Potential symbolic systems to develop:

- Islands and crossings.
- Lanterns, stars, storms, tides, and paths.
- Old ruins and living homes.
- Offerings, inherited objects, and repaired things.
- Songs, names, and remembered phrases.

## Recurring Motifs

Motifs should help the game feel cohesive without becoming heavy-handed.

Track motifs across:

- Dialogue.
- Music.
- Visual design.
- Dungeon landmarks.
- Character animation.
- Quest structure.
- Legends.
- UI and save/rest language, if appropriate.

## Player Journey

The player journey should move through:

1. Familiar warmth.
2. First curiosity.
3. First danger.
4. First proof that the world is larger and older than expected.
5. Growing attachment to companions.
6. Deeper exploration and harder choices.
7. Recontextualized legends.
8. Emotional commitment.
9. Triumph earned through courage, memory, and loyalty.
10. A final feeling of wonder, affection, and remembrance.

## Continuity Rules

1. Preserve approved canon.
2. Do not overwrite the tribute foundation.
3. Character agency must drive major emotional turns.
4. Legends may guide the plot, but they should not remove choice.
5. Every major story location must have history.
6. Every major reveal should connect to prior setup.
7. Humor should remain present even when the story darkens.
8. Plot should never reduce companions to tools for Jay Jay's arc.
9. Spoiler-heavy material should be clearly marked and separated when needed.
10. Supporting-character scenes must pass NAR-001: they should reveal, deepen, challenge, or clarify Jay Jay's emotional journey.
