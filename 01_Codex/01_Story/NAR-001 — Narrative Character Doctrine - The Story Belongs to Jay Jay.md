## Purpose

This doctrine exists to protect the emotional center of *Alamat ni Jay Jay*.

While many characters are inspired by real family members and friends, the story is not about recreating their lives. Their purpose is to enrich Jay Jay's journey and preserve the relationships that shaped him.

## Core Principle

Jay Jay is the protagonist.

Every supporting character exists to deepen the player's understanding of Jay Jay, his growth, his compassion, and the world he experiences.

No supporting character should ever overshadow Jay Jay's emotional journey.

## Character Philosophy

Kuya is inspired by Jeffrey Moralde.

However, Kuya is not Jeffrey.

Kuya is intentionally designed as an older brother archetype so that players experience him through Jay Jay's eyes rather than as a self-insert.

His role is to:

- Protect without controlling.
- Guide without preaching.
- Support without overshadowing.
- Love through actions rather than speeches.

The audience should remember Kuya because of how he influenced Jay Jay, not because he became the hero himself.

## Relationship First

The inspiration for the characters comes from real relationships rather than literal biographies.

The goal is not historical accuracy.

The goal is emotional authenticity.

Whenever a real-life memory is adapted into the game, ask:

"Does this strengthen Jay Jay's story?"

If the answer is no, it does not belong in the game.

If the answer is yes, adapt it into the fantasy world while preserving its emotional truth.

## Creative Guardrail

Never create scenes simply because they showcase Kuya, Nanay, Tay, Kalika, or Kuya Ric.

Every scene should answer one question:

"What does this teach the player about Jay Jay?"

If a supporting character begins attracting the emotional focus away from Jay Jay, revise the scene until Jay Jay once again becomes the emotional center.

## Final Doctrine

*Alamat ni Jay Jay* is a tribute.

Not to one person.

But to a family.

Jay Jay is the heart through which the player experiences that family.

Every relationship should help the player understand why Jay Jay became the person he is.

When in doubt, choose Jay Jay.

## Production Use

Use NAR-001 when reviewing:

- Main story scenes.
- Side stories.
- Character-focused scenes.
- Family-inspired material.
- Tribute moments.
- Dialogue and banter.
- Cinematic staging.
- Opening village moments.
- Marketing story summaries.

Any scene centered on Kuya, Nanay, Tay, Kalika, Kuya Ric, or another supporting character should still identify how the scene deepens Jay Jay's journey.
