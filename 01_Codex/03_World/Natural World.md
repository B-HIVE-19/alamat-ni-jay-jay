## Natural World Design Intent

Nature should feel alive, specific, and consequential. Climate, plants, animals, and terrain should shape architecture, food, travel, combat, and folklore.

## Climate

- Wet season: flooding, fast growth, blocked paths, dangerous river crossings.
- Dry season: clearer routes, exposed reefs, water stress, fire risk.
- Storm season: travel disruption, community preparation, shipwrecks, washed-open ruins.
- Highland climate: mist, cold nights, sudden rain, landslides.
- Volcanic zones: fertile soil, ashfall, mineral springs, sacred fear.

## Flora

| Flora Type | World Use |
| --- | --- |
| Bamboo | Construction, weapons, instruments, bridges. |
| Mangroves | Coastal defense, hidden travel, ecology, taboo spaces. |
| Hardwood Forests | Houses, boats, sacred groves, contested logging. |
| Medicinal Herbs | Healing systems, economy, quests. |
| Rice and Terrace Crops | Food, culture, ritual calendar. |
| Flowering Vines | Visual identity, shrine decoration, poison or medicine. |
| Moss and Ruin Growth | Time depth, hidden inscriptions, dungeon atmosphere. |

## Fauna

Fauna should include ordinary animals, regionally important working animals, dangerous wild animals, and folklore-touched creatures whose behavior people interpret as omens.

## Ecology Rules

1. Creatures need food, shelter, territory, and relationships to local people.
2. Dungeons should have ecology, not random monster placement.
3. Folklore creatures may be rare, misunderstood, territorial, sacred, predatory, or bound to old agreements.
