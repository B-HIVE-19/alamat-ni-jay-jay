## Belief Design Intent

Belief in this world should be practical, emotional, political, and mysterious. People do not all believe the same things in the same way.

## Common Belief Patterns

- Offerings to maintain respect with unseen presences.
- Household rituals tied to ancestors, thresholds, food, and storms.
- Shrine calendars linked to planting, sailing, mourning, and healing.
- Local taboos around trees, rivers, caves, ruins, and certain animals.
- Stories that preserve warnings in exaggerated or symbolic form.

## Myth Design Rules

1. Myths may contradict each other by region.
2. A myth can be spiritually true, historically distorted, politically edited, or completely misunderstood.
3. Dungeons may reveal the material evidence behind a myth without explaining away its mystery.
4. Folklore-inspired beings should have ecological, spiritual, and social meaning.
5. Every legend must have purpose in the living world.
6. Legends should create behavior: offerings, avoidance, festivals, laws, jokes, architecture, route choices, family duties, or political claims.

## Myth Categories

| Category | Function |
| --- | --- |
| Creation Myths | Explain islands, mountains, seas, stars, storms. |
| Warning Tales | Teach boundaries, etiquette, survival, humility. |
| Hero Cycles | Preserve old conflicts and moral contradictions. |
| Spirit Agreements | Explain taboos, offerings, and protected places. |
| Ruin Legends | Distort or preserve ancient history. |

## Legend Purpose Matrix

| Legend Purpose | How It Shapes the World |
| --- | --- |
| Warning | Keeps people away from dangerous terrain, creatures, ruins, or social transgressions. |
| Memory | Preserves a real event after written history has failed or been altered. |
| Law | Justifies a taboo, boundary, punishment, inheritance, or seasonal practice. |
| Navigation | Encodes route knowledge, stars, reefs, weather, or river behavior. |
| Grief | Gives language to loss, disaster, disappearance, or betrayal. |
| Propaganda | Supports a ruler, family claim, shrine mandate, or false history. |
| Bargain | Remembers an agreement with spirits, creatures, ancestors, or old powers. |
| Wonder | Keeps mystery alive and draws people toward pilgrimage, exploration, or art. |
