## Village Design Standard

Villages should not exist only as inns, shops, and quest boards. Each village needs a reason for being where it is: food, water, safety, trade, ritual duty, resource access, exile, defense, or ancestral claim.

## Village Index

| Village Type | Location Logic | Economy | Social Texture |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Stilt Fishing Village | Mangrove edge, reef lagoon, river mouth | Fish, salt, shellcraft, ferry work | Practical, weather-aware, rumor-rich |
| Terrace Village | Mountain slope, spring source, old path | Rice, root crops, herbs, woven goods | Patient, ritual calendar, strong kinship ties |
| River Crossing Village | Bridge, ford, ferry, shrine bend | Tolls, food trade, boat repair | Talkative, politically aware, suspicious of strangers |
| Shrine Village | Sacred grove, ruin gate, spirit boundary | Offerings, pilgrimage, ritual services | Formal etiquette, old stories, local taboos |
| Market Village | Road and river junction | Mixed goods, livestock, cloth, tools | Noisy, funny, competitive, full of news |

## Local Customs

- Offering before cutting certain trees, crossing certain bridges, or entering old paths.
- Shared storm preparation as a community duty.
- Guest seating that reveals status and trust.
- Naming days after weather, harvest, or ancestral events.
- Public storytelling nights where history and gossip blur.

## Key NPCs

Each settlement should define:

- A practical authority.
- A spiritual or cultural authority.
- A person who knows the road, river, or sea.
- A person who benefits from the current problem.
- A person who quietly remembers what others deny.
