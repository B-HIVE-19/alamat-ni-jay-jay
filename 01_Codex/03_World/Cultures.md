## Cultural Design Intent

Cultures should be local, practical, expressive, and historically layered. Inspiration may come from Filipino cultural textures, but every culture in the game world must remain original to *Alamat ni Jay Jay*.

## Traditions

- Storm preparation rituals.
- Shared harvest work.
- Boat blessing before dangerous crossings.
- Story nights after market days.
- Mourning practices tied to water, trees, or household objects.
- Coming-of-age tasks based on local survival skills.

## Beliefs

- Ancestors remain socially present through names, objects, stories, and obligations.
- Some places require permission, silence, offerings, or avoidance.
- Spirits and creatures may be treated as neighbors, dangers, ancestors, tests, or mysteries depending on region.
- Ruins are not universally viewed the same way; they may be sacred, cursed, profitable, illegal, or scholarly.

## Conflicts

- Trade towns versus inland villages over taxes and access.
- Elders versus younger navigators, merchants, or scholars.
- Shrine keepers versus relic hunters.
- Mountain communities versus lowland rulers.
- Families divided by old ruin claims.
- Practical survival needs versus spiritual restrictions.
