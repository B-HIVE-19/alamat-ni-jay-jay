## Design Intent

Natural resources should make the world feel interconnected. Resources determine where people settle, what they trade, what they fight over, what they protect, and what they believe should never be touched.

No resource should exist only as crafting material. Major resources should affect economy, politics, architecture, transportation, religion, dungeon access, and local identity.

## Resource Categories

| Resource Type | Examples | World Function |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Food Staples | Rice, root crops, fruit, dried fish, salt | Survival, trade stability, festivals, shortages. |
| Building Materials | Bamboo, hardwood, stone, thatch, shell, clay | Architecture, shipbuilding, bridges, tools. |
| Trade Goods | Pearls, woven cloth, ceramics, carved goods, medicinal plants | Status, commerce, diplomacy, quests. |
| Strategic Materials | Metal ore, volcanic glass, rare hardwood, boat timber | Political control, weapons, tools, guarded routes. |
| Sacred Materials | Shrine woods, ritual stones, offering plants, ancestral relics | Taboos, pilgrimages, religious authority. |
| Dangerous Materials | Poison plants, unstable minerals, cursed relics, storm glass | Dungeons, restricted trade, fear, black markets. |

## Resource Design Rules

1. Every major resource needs a source region.
2. Every valuable resource should create labor, trade, protection, or conflict.
3. Sacred resources should have taboos or rituals.
4. Scarcity should affect politics and daily life.
5. Resource extraction should leave visible marks on settlements and landscapes.
