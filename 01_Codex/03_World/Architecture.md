## Design Intent

Architecture should make the world feel lived in, old, climate-aware, and culturally specific without directly recreating historical Philippines.

Buildings should reveal how people respond to heat, rain, storms, floods, spirits, social rank, local materials, trade wealth, and ancient ruins.

## Architectural Rules

1. Buildings must respond to climate and terrain.
2. Materials should come from local ecology and trade.
3. Sacred, civic, domestic, and military spaces should feel different.
4. Ancient architecture should influence present-day construction in visible but imperfect ways.
5. Architecture should create gameplay: alleys, bridges, raised walkways, courtyards, docks, terraces, rooflines, thresholds, gates, and hidden understructures.
6. No settlement should look generic; silhouette, material, layout, and ornament should reveal place.

## Common Design Elements

| Element | Use |
| --- | --- |
| Raised Floors | Flood protection, airflow, animal storage, social boundaries. |
| Steep Roofs | Heavy rain, heat release, strong silhouettes. |
| Wide Eaves | Shade, rain protection, market shelter. |
| Bamboo and Hardwood Frames | Local craft, flexibility, class variation. |
| Woven Panels and Shutters | Ventilation, privacy, pattern identity. |
| Stilt Walkways | Mangrove, river, and floodplain settlements. |
| Stone Terraces | Ancient foundations, agriculture, shrine approaches. |
| Carved Posts | Family marks, guardian symbols, local humor, warnings. |
| Rope Bridges | Mountain routes, risk, local maintenance obligations. |
| Courtyards | Family gatherings, craft work, ritual, gossip. |
| Boat Yards | Coastal economy, repair culture, travel preparation. |
| Shrine Thresholds | Offerings, etiquette, supernatural caution. |

## Settlement Architecture by Region

| Region | Architectural Identity |
| --- | --- |
| Coastal Trade Belt | Docks, boatyards, salt sheds, warehouses, raised markets, bright shutters, storm shutters. |
| River Interior | Houses near crossings, granaries, bridge shrines, irrigation gates, communal work spaces. |
| Volcanic Highlands | Stone retaining walls, steep paths, mineral baths, watch platforms, ash-resistant roofs. |
| Mangrove Labyrinth | Stilt clusters, narrow plankways, hidden moorings, root-wrapped shrines. |
| Outer Islands | Wind-braced homes, cliff paths, signal towers, shipwreck salvage structures. |
| Ancestral Ruin Zones | New homes built against old stone, forbidden foundations, reused carved blocks, contested sacred gates. |

## Ancient Architecture

Ancient structures should suggest civilizations with engineering, astronomy, water management, ritual space, language, and political control.

Ancient architecture may include:

- Terraced ceremonial platforms.
- Tide-aligned causeways.
- Sealed chambers beneath ordinary settlements.
- Star or storm observatories.
- Flooded civic plazas.
- Stone channels for water, sound, or light.
- Gateways whose meaning has been forgotten.
