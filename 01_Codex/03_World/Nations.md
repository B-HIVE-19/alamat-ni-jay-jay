## Design Intent

Nations and regional powers in *Alamat ni Jay Jay* should feel native to an island world shaped by weather, sea routes, river mouths, ancestral claims, shrine authority, trade debt, and ancient ruins.

This is not historical Philippines and not generic medieval fantasy. Political power should feel local, maritime, personal, and old.

## Power Design Rules

1. A kingdom or power must grow from geography.
2. Authority should be contested, negotiated, inherited, performed, or ritually justified.
3. No ruler should control everything cleanly.
4. Ports, ferries, guilds, shrine keepers, elders, and ruin claimants may rival formal rulers.
5. Political borders should be less important than routes, obligations, tribute, marriage ties, seasonal access, and reputation.
6. Every political power should have a founding story that may be partly true.

## Draft Power Types

| Power Type | Basis of Authority | World Function |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Island Polity | Control of one island, harbor, or agricultural basin | Local identity, travel permissions, defense. |
| River Kingdom | Control of river routes, terraces, irrigation, and crossings | Food supply, inland trade, shrine calendars. |
| Sail House Confederation | Alliances between navigator families and boat guilds | Inter-island travel, maps, rescue, blockade. |
| Shrine Mandate | Religious authority tied to sacred sites or spirit agreements | Legitimacy, taboos, festivals, access to ruins. |
| Port League | Merchant houses, docks, tolls, warehouses, and debt | Trade pressure, politics, information flow. |
| Highland Protectorate | Mountain passes, mineral sources, old fortifications | Isolation, military leverage, ancient secrets. |
| Ruin Claim Lineage | Claimed descent or stewardship over ancient sites | Dungeon access, relic disputes, history control. |

## Open Questions

- Is there one major realm, several island powers, or a loose network of competing authorities?
- Does Jay Jay begin inside a politically central place or a marginal one?
- Which power benefits most from keeping ancient history fragmented?
- Which political authority is most threatened by living folklore being proven true?
