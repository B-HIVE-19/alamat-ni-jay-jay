## Political Design Intent

Power in *Alamat ni Jay Jay* should feel local, layered, and negotiated. Authority should come from land, sea routes, family history, ritual duty, wealth, age, knowledge, and control of resources.

## Authority Types

| Authority | Power Source | Story Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Village Council | Kinship, age, land knowledge | Local disputes, tradition, conservative pressure. |
| Port Authority | Trade, docks, taxes, permits | Travel access, corruption, market conflict. |
| Boat Guild | Navigation, ship repair, route knowledge | Transport, secrets, rescue, blockade. |
| Shrine Keepers | Ritual knowledge, sacred obligations | Myth, taboo, legitimacy, spiritual warnings. |
| Merchant Houses | Wealth, storage, debt, supply chains | Political leverage, patronage, exploitation. |
| Regional Protector | Military defense or inherited mandate | Larger conflicts, borders, law. |
| Ruin Claimants | Ancestry, scholarship, force, or faith | Dungeon access, relic disputes, historical truth. |

## Political Texture

Politics should affect daily life: who may dock, who pays tolls, who can enter a ruin, who owns a bridge, who receives help after a storm, and whose version of history is taught.
