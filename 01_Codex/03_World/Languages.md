## Language Design Intent

Language should make the world feel old, regional, and interconnected without becoming a direct copy of any real language. Names should support tone, memory, place, and culture.

## Naming

### Naming Principles

- Place names may come from terrain, founder stories, old spirits, disasters, crops, animals, or navigational features.
- Personal names may reveal family history, birth circumstances, faith, aspiration, or regional identity.
- Ancient names may survive in shortened, mispronounced, or ritual-only forms.

## Common Phrases

This section will track approved phrases, greetings, farewells, ritual language, market slang, insults, jokes, and relationship-specific speech.

## Translation Notes

- Use italicized local terms sparingly and with context.
- Avoid overloading dialogue with glossary language.
- Let meaning emerge through scene, response, and repeated usage.
- Preserve approved terms once established.
