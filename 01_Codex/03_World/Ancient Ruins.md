## Ruin Design Intent

Ancient ruins should prove that the world existed long before the present story. They should carry architecture, engineering, religion, language, and political consequences from civilizations that are gone, misunderstood, or deliberately hidden.

## Ruin Design Rules

1. Every ruin had an original purpose.
2. Every ruin has a present-day interpretation that may be incomplete or wrong.
3. Ruins should affect local politics, religion, economy, and danger.
4. Ruin layouts should reflect ancient function before dungeon needs.
5. Relics should raise questions about ownership, memory, and responsibility.
6. Every ruin should reveal something about the civilization that made it: what they feared, valued, controlled, measured, worshiped, or hid.
7. A ruin should never exist only as a dungeon shell.

## Ruin Types

| Ruin Type | Original Function | Present-Day Tension |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Terrace Complex | Agriculture, ritual calendar, water control | Food politics, sacred access, buried chambers. |
| Stone Causeway | Inter-island travel, ceremony, defense | Tidal routes, smuggling, pilgrimage. |
| Observatory Shrine | Stars, seasons, navigation, omens | Navigator guild secrets, prophecy disputes. |
| Sealed Archive | Memory preservation, law, lineage, magic | Scholars, rulers, and thieves compete for access. |
| Flooded City | Trade, governance, disaster site | Salvage, mourning, dangerous ecology. |
| Mountain Fortress | Defense, exile, ritual protection | Regional claims, old weapons, hidden prisoners or ghosts. |

## Ancient Civilization Design Axes

Use these axes when developing ancient cultures:

| Axis | Design Question |
| --- | --- |
| Water | Did they control rivers, tides, rain, wells, or floodplains? |
| Sky | Did they track stars, storms, seasons, omens, or navigation? |
| Memory | Did they preserve history through stone, song, knots, masks, or ritual roles? |
| Power | Who was allowed to enter sacred or civic spaces? |
| Trade | What did they move between islands, and who controlled the routes? |
| Folklore | Were later legends born from their technology, rituals, disasters, or agreements? |
| Collapse | Did they fall through war, flood, betrayal, spiritual rupture, migration, or deliberate concealment? |

## Open Questions

- Were the First Builders human, partly supernatural, or a coalition of cultures?
- Did they fall, vanish, transform, or deliberately hide?
- What truth about the present world is buried in their ruins?
