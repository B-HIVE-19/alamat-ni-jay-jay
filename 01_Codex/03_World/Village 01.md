## Purpose

Village 01 is the first production-tracked settlement candidate.

This document does not approve the village name, location, culture, story role, or layout. It defines the production slot and the questions the village must answer before approval.

## Design Requirements

Village 01 must:

- Feel like a real place that existed before Jay Jay arrived.
- Support the opening sense of wonder, warmth, curiosity, and danger beyond the edge of safety.
- Contain daily life, humor, local customs, family texture, gossip, work, weather awareness, and history.
- Connect to at least one route, one legend, one local problem, and one future dungeon or exploration hook.
- Make the player want to leave and return.

## The Great Balete — Emotional Center of the Village

From the mockup sheet:

More than a landmark, it is where life happens. Children play. Elders rest. Fishermen watch the sea. Stories are shared. Festivals come alive. Memories are made.

**It is not mystical. It is home.**

(Design note: balete trees are traditionally engkanto-haunted in Filipino folklore — making the village balete deliberately *not* mystical is a strong, intentional subversion that reserves the mystical baletes for the wild. Worth confirming as intended.)

## Village Layout (from overview map)

Coastal village wrapped around the Great Balete, with docks on the water.

1. The Great Balete (Village Center)
2. Jay Jay's House
3. Kuya & Kuya Ric's Workshop (shared by Kuya, CHR-002, and Kuya Ric — both House Moralde)
4. Nanay's Kitchen (House)
5. The Dock / Fishermen Area
6. Market Stalls
7. Community Path / Main Road

Key locations close to the tree:

- **Jay Jay's House** — just a few steps from the Balete. Easy to return to, easy to run back out.
- **Kuya & Kuya Ric's Workshop** — where things get fixed, improved, invented, and where Kuya Ric is always half in, half out.
- **Nanay's Kitchen** — the heart of the home. The smell of food is the soundtrack of the village.
- **The Dock** — fishermen leave early. Fishermen return with stories, fish, and laughter.

## Daily Life Flow

- Morning: children play, people gather, Nanay starts cooking.
- Noon: market opens, fishermen return, stories and trading.
- Afternoon: rest, chores, repairs, lessons, quiet moments.
- Evening: shared meals, conversations, laughter.
- Night: lanterns dim; waves, wind, crickets.

## First Hour Experience

- Explore the village.
- Meet the family.
- Help with small tasks.
- Learn the people.
- Feel at home.

## Emotional Goal

The player should think:

> "These people are wonderful."

Not:

> "Okay… where's the dungeon?"

Tagline from sheet: **Life First. Family Always. Home is Where the Story Begins.**

## Art Direction Notes (from sheet)

- Warm and vibrant color palette.
- Hand-painted textures.
- Natural materials (wood, stone, thatch).
- Lush but not overwhelming.
- Life over spectacle.
