## Island Design Intent

Each island should feel like a complete place with its own terrain, settlements, economy, dangers, beliefs, routes, and secrets. Islands should not be treated as isolated level hubs; they should affect one another through trade, migration, politics, marriage, rumor, storms, and old debts.

## Island Profile Template

| Field | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Island Name | Working or approved name. |
| Region Type | Coastal, highland, outer island, ruin zone, etc. |
| Terrain | Dominant geography and traversal challenges. |
| Major Settlements | Villages, cities, ports, shrine sites. |
| Resources | Food, materials, craft goods, rare resources. |
| Political Control | Who claims authority and who actually has power. |
| Beliefs and Taboos | Local myths, rituals, forbidden places. |
| Travel Links | Boats, ferries, reefs, bridges, causeways. |
