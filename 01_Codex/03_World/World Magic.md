## Design Intent

Magic in Alamat should feel native to the world rather than pasted onto it as a generic RPG system.

Magic should connect to folklore, spirits, memory, nature, ancient civilizations, ritual practice, healing, danger, social responsibility, and the emotional heart of the journey.

## World Magic Principles

1. Magic should have cultural meaning, not only combat utility.
2. Magic should affect how people build, travel, heal, fear, worship, and remember.
3. Magic should be readable for gameplay while retaining mystery in the world.
4. Different regions may understand magic differently.
5. Magic should create obligations, taboos, costs, or consequences.
6. Ancient magic, if present, should be treated with caution and historical weight.

## Magic in Society

| Area | Possible Role |
| --- | --- |
| Healing | Practical medicine, ritual care, rare gifts, village trust. |
| Navigation | Weather signs, tide rituals, route songs, star interpretation. |
| Protection | Wards, offerings, boundary markers, house rituals. |
| Agriculture | Planting calendars, water rites, fertility beliefs. |
| Dungeons | Seals, puzzles, old mechanisms, creature bonds, hidden rooms. |
| Politics | Shrine legitimacy, forbidden knowledge, controlled relics. |
| Daily Life | Small rituals, charms, etiquette, inherited caution. |

## Relationship to Gameplay Magic

World magic and gameplay magic should support each other.

See `../05_Gameplay/Magic.md` for player-facing systems. World Magic defines cultural, historical, and social logic.

## Open Questions

- Is magic common, rare, feared, regulated, inherited, learned, or gifted?
- Who teaches magic?
- What does magic cost emotionally, socially, spiritually, or physically?
- How do ordinary people distinguish magic, folklore, trickery, and ancient technology?
