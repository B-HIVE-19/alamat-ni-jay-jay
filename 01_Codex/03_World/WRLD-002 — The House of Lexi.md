## Purpose

Establish the House of Lexi as the protected merchant hospitality standard for *Alamat ni Jay Jay*.

A House of Lexi should not feel like a generic shop. It should be a place of welcome, trust, memory, trade, hospitality, and story exchange.

## Core Motto

Take Only What You Need.

Leave With More Than You Came For.

## The House of Lexi

A House of Lexi is more than a merchant.

It is a place of welcome.

A place of trust.

A place where travelers become friends.

A place where stories are exchanged as freely as Vyra.

No customer should ever leave with only a purchase.

They should leave carrying something worth remembering.

## Design Philosophy

The House of Lexi exists to make commerce feel human.

Buying supplies should not feel like interacting with an anonymous vendor screen. It should feel like stepping into a place where travelers are recognized, stories matter, and generosity has social meaning.

The House of Lexi should support:

- Warmth.
- Trust.
- Hospitality.
- Memory.
- Story exchange.
- Community connection.
- Practical care for travelers.
- Commerce with emotional texture.

## Gameplay and UI Use

Use the House of Lexi standard when developing:

- Shops.
- Merchant dialogue.
- Supply purchases.
- Item descriptions.
- Shop UI.
- Travel preparation.
- Inn/shop hybrid spaces, if approved later.
- Price, gift, discount, favor, or debt interactions.
- Story moments involving commerce.

Every House of Lexi interaction should ask:

"Does the player leave with more than an item?"

That "more" may be a story, joke, rumor, kindness, memory, local warning, small gift, relationship beat, or sense of home.

## Economic Relationship

Vyra remains the canonical currency used in House of Lexi transactions.

However, the House of Lexi should remind the player that value in Alamat is not only financial.

Stories, trust, warnings, reputation, kindness, and hospitality can matter as much as price.

## Protected Canon

A House of Lexi is more than a merchant.

Its guiding motto is:

"Take Only What You Need. Leave With More Than You Came For."

Future shops, merchant spaces, commerce UI, merchant dialogue, travel supply systems, and economy-facing moments should preserve the House of Lexi as a place of welcome, trust, and memory rather than reducing it to a transactional vendor.
