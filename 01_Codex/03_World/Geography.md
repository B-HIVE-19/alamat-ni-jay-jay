## Geographic Design Intent

Geography should drive story, economy, politics, dungeon design, and travel. The island world should feel shaped by water, mountains, storms, reefs, forests, rivers, and ancient routes.

## Landmarks

- Sacred mountains visible from several regions.
- River mouths where trade towns grow.
- Reef gates that decide safe passage.
- Mangrove borders that hide alternate routes.
- Old causeways exposed by tides.
- Terraces built over older stonework.
- Volcanic highlands that provide fertile soil and danger.

## Travel Routes

- Coastal routes between ports and fishing settlements.
- River routes into agricultural interiors.
- Mountain passes connecting isolated hamlets.
- Mangrove channels known only to local navigators.
- Ancient causeways used as shortcuts when conditions are right.

## Natural Hazards

- Typhoons.
- Flash floods.
- Landslides.
- Reef wreckage.
- Volcanic ash.
- Poisonous plants.
- Predator territories.
- Spirit-taboo zones where danger may be natural, supernatural, or social.
