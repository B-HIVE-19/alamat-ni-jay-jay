## Design Intent

Technology in Alamat should feel practical, handmade, regional, and shaped by climate, geography, trade, materials, and ancient inheritance.

WRLD-001 establishes Alamat as a modern fantasy society for economic life, banking, digital payments, salaries, military payroll, commercial trade, taxation, and daily transactions.

The world should support classic JRPG adventure without defaulting to generic medieval fantasy or letting modern convenience erase travel, danger, distance, uncertainty, or local texture.

## Technology Principles

1. Tools should come from local needs: storms, boats, rivers, terraces, forests, mountains, ruins, and trade.
2. Ancient technology may exist, but should feel mysterious, rare, misunderstood, or ritually controlled.
3. Technology should not erase adventure. Travel, darkness, danger, distance, and uncertainty must still matter.
4. Craft traditions should vary by region.
5. Everyday tools should reveal daily life.

## Ancient Technology

Ancient technology should raise questions:

- Was it engineering, magic, ritual, or all three?
- Who understands it now?
- Who is allowed to use it?
- What danger comes from misuse?
- What myths formed around it?

## Open Questions

- How advanced were the First Builders?
- Are ancient mechanisms common in dungeons?
- Does the player use maps, compasses, route songs, or learned landmarks?
- What technology belongs in towns but not dungeons?
- What form do digital Vyra payments take in UI, shops, banks, and military payroll?
