## Core Read

Jay Jay is the emotional center of the story. His final characterization must feel specific, lived-in, and human before he is treated as a protagonist or combat unit.

Jay Jay is an original fictional hero created as a tribute, not a biographical recreation. He is inspired by the qualities Jeffrey loved most about his late brother.

In the world of *Alamat ni Jay Jay*, Jay Jay becomes the hero he always dreamed of being.

CHR-001A, The Heart of Jay Jay, is the protected character doctrine for Jay Jay's emotional authenticity. Jay Jay should be kind, humble, creative, curious, compassionate, observant, funny with family, imperfect, and unforgettable. His sketchbook, high-pitched voice, fast excited speech, dry humor, The Face, moonwalk attempts, mimicry, and collected joy are protected characterization signals.

## If Jay Jay Never Existed

This question is not applicable in the same way for Jay Jay because he is the title character, but the design standard still applies: Jay Jay must have a believable life, routine, relationships, fears, habits, and future outside the demands of the plot.

## Personal History

Known canon:

- Jay Jay is inspired by Jeffrey Moralde's late brother.
- Jay Jay is not a biographical recreation.
- Jay Jay is an original fictional character inspired by loved qualities, memory, and spirit.
- In this world, Jay Jay becomes the hero he always dreamed of being.
- Jay Jay is not designed to become a perfect hero; he is designed to become unforgettable.
- Jay Jay sketches the world because drawing teaches him to truly see it.
- Jay Jay's kindness is a choice, not naivete.
- Jay Jay's closest family may see his dry humor, The Face, moonwalk attempts, mimicry, and joyful imperfections.

## Tribute Guardrails

- Protect the emotional authenticity of Jay Jay above spectacle.
- Do not use Jay Jay to recreate real-life events unless explicitly approved.
- Do not reduce Jay Jay to a symbol; he must still feel like a full person.
- Fictionalize with respect, warmth, and care.
- When developing Jay Jay, ask: "Does this honor the spirit of the project?"
