## Purpose

This document exists to protect the emotional authenticity of Jay Jay throughout the development of *Alamat ni Jay Jay*.

Jay Jay is not designed to become a perfect hero.

He is designed to become unforgettable.

## Who Jay Jay Is

Jay Jay is kind.

Jay Jay is humble.

Jay Jay is creative.

Jay Jay is curious.

Jay Jay is compassionate.

Jay Jay trusts people until they prove otherwise.

He notices details other people miss.

He sketches the world because drawing teaches him to truly see it.

## First Impressions

People often underestimate Jay Jay.

He looks much younger than he really is.

His voice is naturally high-pitched.

When he gets excited, he talks too fast.

His words begin running together.

Sometimes people ask him to repeat himself.

He simply smiles and starts over.

He is never embarrassed by who he is.

## The Truth

The greatest mistake people make is believing Jay Jay is naive.

He is not.

He simply chooses kindness first.

Players should experience the exact same journey.

At first:

"This kid?"

Later:

"I understand now."

## The Bitch Factor

Jay Jay is not perfect.

He has attitude.

Not toward strangers.

Toward the people he loves.

Only Nanay.

Only Kuya.

Only Kuya Ric.

Only Kalika.

Only family.

When they tease him long enough, Jay Jay responds with perfectly timed dry humor or The Face.

## The Face

The Face is legendary.

No dialogue is required.

One raised eyebrow.

One exhausted stare.

One silent expression that says:

"I am done with all of you."

The people closest to him immediately burst into laughter.

Because they know he is not angry.

He is home.

## The Moonwalk

When Jay Jay is genuinely happy, he instinctively attempts to moonwalk.

Nobody asked him to.

Nobody taught him.

He simply believes:

"This one is going to be perfect."

Sometimes it is.

Usually, it is not.

He never stops trying.

That optimism is part of his charm.

## The Mimic

Jay Jay loves people.

He also loves the things that make people laugh.

If he sees a funny dance, he tries it.

If he hears a funny saying, he repeats it.

If someone has an interesting habit, he unconsciously copies it.

He collects joy the same way he collects sketches.

Not because he wants attention.

Because happiness is contagious.

## The Sketchbook

The sketchbook is not a hobby.

It is part of Jay Jay.

He records places.

People.

Expressions.

Memories.

He places it beneath his pillow every night.

It is the last thing he protects before he sleeps.

## The Player's Journey

Players should never admire Jay Jay because he is powerful.

Players should love Jay Jay because he feels real.

His kindness.

His awkwardness.

His high voice.

His mumbling.

His moonwalk.

His dry humor.

His compassion.

His curiosity.

His imperfections.

These are not flaws.

They are fingerprints.

## The Final Test

Every scene involving Jay Jay should answer one question:

"Does this feel like Jay Jay?"

If the answer is no, rewrite the scene.

## Final Doctrine

The goal is not for players to admire Jay Jay.

The goal is for players to miss him when the game is over.

If players finish *Alamat ni Jay Jay* and find themselves smiling because they remember:

- The moonwalk.
- The Face.
- The mumbling.
- The sketchbook.
- The pumpkin sweets.
- The egg rolls.
- The laughter.

Then Jay Jay's legacy has been preserved.

That is the true victory of *Alamat ni Jay Jay*.
