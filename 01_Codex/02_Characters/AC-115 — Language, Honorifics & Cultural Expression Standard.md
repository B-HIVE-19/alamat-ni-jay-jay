## Purpose

This document establishes the narrative standards governing the use of Filipino-inspired language, honorifics, family titles, and cultural expressions throughout *Alamat ni Jay Jay*.

This is not a translation document.

This is not a language guide.

It is a narrative consistency document.

Its purpose is to ensure that Filipino cultural influence feels authentic, natural, emotionally meaningful, and accessible to players around the world while preserving *Alamat ni Jay Jay* as an original fantasy world.

Language should communicate relationships before vocabulary.

Every Filipino-inspired word used in the game should carry emotional meaning. Players should naturally understand each word through context without requiring definitions. The goal is immersion rather than education.

## Core Philosophy

Do not overuse Filipino words.

Use them intentionally.

Every retained Filipino-inspired word should strengthen:

- Family.
- Respect.
- Friendship.
- Community.
- Emotional connection.

Words should become part of the player's emotional vocabulary because the player experiences what they mean through relationship, context, repetition, and care.

## Canon Terms

### Nanay

Definition:

Household mother. The woman who raises the family. Biological relationship is secondary.

Narrative Meaning:

- Home.
- Safety.
- Comfort.
- Unconditional love.

Canonical Rule:

Jay Jay's biological grandmother is called "Nanay."

She fulfilled the role of mother after Jay Jay lost his own mother.

Jay Jay never calls her "Lola."

Kuya also calls her "Nanay."

Much of the village affectionately knows her as "Nanay."

This reflects her role within both the family and the community.

### Kuya

Definition:

Older brother. Respected older male. Guide. Protector.

Narrative Meaning:

- Responsibility.
- Leadership.
- Trust.

Canonical Rule:

Jay Jay refers to his biological older brother simply as "Kuya."

For much of the early game, players know him only by this title.

His personal name is intentionally secondary to the relationship.

Kuya Ric is addressed by his full nickname because it identifies him as both a respected older-brother figure and an individual character.

### Kuya Ric

Narrative Role:

- Family friend.
- Best friend of Kuya.
- Older-brother figure to Jay Jay.
- Community protector.

Signature Expression:

"Tara."

This phrase is used sparingly and intentionally.

It represents confidence, leadership, and moving forward together.

It must never be reduced to a repetitive catchphrase.

### Ate

Reserved for future characters.

Represents a respected older sister figure.

Used only when emotionally appropriate.

## Language Design Principles

Dialogue should remain primarily in English.

Selected Filipino-inspired words should remain untranslated when:

- Emotional meaning is stronger than literal translation.
- Relationship is more important than definition.
- Context naturally teaches the player.

Avoid overwhelming players with unfamiliar vocabulary.

Every retained Filipino expression should feel welcoming rather than confusing.

The player should not need a glossary to feel the emotional meaning of a scene.

## Cultural Expression

Filipino inspiration should primarily appear through lived values, behavior, and relationships.

These include:

- Family relationships.
- Hospitality.
- Community support.
- Respect for elders.
- Shared meals.
- Humor.
- Music.
- Celebration.
- Daily life.
- Acts of kindness.

These values should be experienced rather than explained.

## Avoid

Do not insert Filipino vocabulary merely for flavor.

Do not stereotype culture.

Do not require players to memorize terminology.

Do not interrupt immersion with explanations.

Do not treat word count as cultural authenticity.

Cultural authenticity comes from relationships and values rather than vocabulary density.

## Creative Review Questions

Before introducing any Filipino-inspired term, ask:

- Does this strengthen character relationships?
- Would the scene lose emotional meaning if translated into English?
- Can players understand it naturally through context?
- Does it support the emotional heart of *Alamat ni Jay Jay*?

If the answer is no, prefer clear English.

## Narrative Director Notes

*Alamat ni Jay Jay* is not attempting to teach a language.

It is inviting players into a family.

Words such as "Nanay," "Kuya," and carefully selected expressions are preserved because they communicate relationships that cannot be fully replaced by direct English equivalents.

The player should come to understand these words not because they are explained, but because they experience them.

## Canon Decisions

- Jay Jay calls his grandmother "Nanay."
- Kuya also calls her "Nanay."
- Nanay is recognized throughout the village as the family matriarch.
- Jay Jay's older brother is introduced by role before personal name.
- Kuya Ric retains "Tara." as his signature expression.
- Filipino-inspired language is used intentionally and sparingly.
- Cultural expression should be carried by relationships, daily life, hospitality, humor, music, shared meals, and acts of kindness rather than vocabulary density.
