## Purpose

Character Lock documents are the official cross-discipline reference for major characters.

Once approved, a Character Lock becomes the source used by artists, writers, programmers, animators, combat designers, UI designers, and audio designers. Locked material should not be changed without an official revision.

## Lock Rules

1. Do not mark a character lock as approved until all major fields are reviewed.
2. Do not invent permanent traits to fill blank sections.
3. Preserve existing canon.
4. If a proposed detail conflicts with established canon, flag it before approval.
5. Combat style must come after personality, values, flaws, and arc.
6. Visual identity must support personality, history, and world logic.
