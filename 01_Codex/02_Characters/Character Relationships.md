## Relationship Design Standard

Relationships in *Alamat ni Jay Jay* should evolve naturally over the story. Characters should disagree, misunderstand each other, repair trust, share private jokes, and change how they speak to one another as they gain history together.

No relationship should exist only to explain a party role or feed Jay Jay's arc. Every major relationship should reveal something about both people involved.

Relationships should not remain static. They should accumulate memory: old arguments, favors, debts, jokes, embarrassment, loyalty tests, and quiet changes in how characters speak to each other.

## Tensions

Tensions should come from personality, values, history, obligations, fear, pride, love, guilt, loyalty, or incompatible truths. Avoid tension that exists only to create party drama.

## Alliances

Alliances should have reasons beyond convenience. Track what each person gives, receives, hides, misunderstands, or sacrifices.

## Private Languages

This section tracks running jokes, shorthand, nicknames, shared rituals, and small behavioral details that make relationships feel lived in.

From art sheets (draft, pending approval):

- Nobody calls Jay Jay "Jay Jay." It's always "Hey Jay" or "#2." He knows. He accepts it. (Ishi sheet)
- Kuya Ric's catchphrase: "Tara. I'll go first." ("Tara" — let's go.)
- Ishi's shop greeting doubles as teasing: "Need your blade sharpened? …Or are you just here to hear it again, Hey Jay?"
- House of Lexi motto: "Take Only What You Need. Leave With More Than You Came For."
- Pandi always retrieves the wrong item first (a fish), then somehow produces exactly what's needed. (Lexi storyboard)
- MJ-23 calls Mark "Partner."

## Dialogue Evolution

As relationships change, dialogue should change with them. Characters may become more direct, more teasing, more protective, more honest, less formal, or more willing to sit in silence together.
