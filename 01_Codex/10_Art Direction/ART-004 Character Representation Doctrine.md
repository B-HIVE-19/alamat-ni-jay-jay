## Purpose

*Alamat ni Jay Jay* is an original fantasy world inspired by real people, real relationships, and real memories.

The goal is never to recreate real life exactly.

The goal is to preserve emotional truth.

## Core Principle

Characters exist on a spectrum between inspiration and representation.

The amount a character visually resembles their real-life inspiration depends entirely on their narrative role.

## Relationship to ART-005

ART-005, Character Artwork Recognition Standard, defines how artists preserve recognizable identity within the tier boundaries established by ART-004.

ART-004 answers:

"How literal may this character's resemblance be?"

ART-005 answers:

"How do we make the artwork feel recognizably like them?"

ART-005 does not override ART-004. Tier I characters should remain original JRPG heroes rather than direct portraits, even when ART-005 asks artists to protect emotional recognition, expression, body language, and key identity signals.

## Tier I - The Main Four

- Jay Jay.
- Kalika.
- Kuya.
- Mark.

These characters carry the emotional weight of the entire story.

Players spend dozens of hours with them.

Because of this, they should become fully realized fantasy characters rather than literal recreations of their real-life inspirations.

Their personalities, values, relationships, humor, and quirks should all remain emotionally authentic.

Their visual identity, however, belongs to the world of Alamat.

Players should see them as original JRPG heroes.

Not direct portraits of real people.

## Tier II - Family and Supporting Cast

Examples:

- Nanay.
- Tay.
- Josie.
- Kuya Ric.
- Lexi.
- Pandi.

These characters appear in shorter moments throughout the game.

Since players experience them in glimpses rather than living their journey, they may resemble their real-life inspirations much more closely.

The goal is simple:

Family members should immediately smile and recognize them without disrupting the fantasy world.

## Tier III - Cameos

- Friends.
- Extended family.
- Special guests.
- Recurring villagers.
- Optional NPCs.

These characters may closely resemble their real-life counterparts.

They exist as affectionate acknowledgements and celebrations rather than central narrative figures.

## Character Design Philosophy

The closer a character is to the player's point of view, the more original they become.

The farther a character is from the player's point of view, the more they may resemble the real person who inspired them.

This balance allows players to fully become Jay Jay while allowing family and friends to recognize themselves throughout the world.

## Emotional Goal

Players should experience the world through Jay Jay.

Family should experience moments of recognition.

Neither should compromise the other.

## Final Doctrine

*Alamat ni Jay Jay* is not a biography.

It is not historical fiction.

It is not autobiography.

It is an alternative fantasy reality inspired by real love, real friendships, real family, and real memories.

Every character should preserve the emotional truth of the person who inspired them while remaining fully at home within the world of Alamat.

The objective is not perfect resemblance.

The objective is perfect recognition.

## Production Use

Use ART-004 when developing:

- Character concept art.
- Character locks.
- Portraits.
- Sprites.
- Cinematics.
- Marketing character art.
- Cameo NPCs.
- Family and supporting cast likeness guidance.
- Any character inspired by a real person.

## Representation Tier Summary

| Tier | Character Type | Visual Resemblance Rule | Emotional Rule |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Tier I | Main Four | Fully realized original fantasy heroes; avoid direct portrait recreation. | Preserve emotional truth, values, humor, quirks, and relationships. |
| Tier II | Family and Supporting Cast | May resemble inspirations more closely while staying native to Alamat. | Create recognition without disrupting the fantasy world. |
| Tier III | Cameos | May closely resemble real-life counterparts. | Function as affectionate acknowledgements and celebrations. |
