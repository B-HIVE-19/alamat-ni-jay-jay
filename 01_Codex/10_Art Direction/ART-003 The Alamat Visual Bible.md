## ID Assignment Note

The source request named this document ART-001. ART-001 is already assigned to the Main Character Production Bible, and ART-002 is already assigned to the Artistic Direction Bible. To preserve the official registry and avoid a canonical ID collision, this Visual Bible is assigned ART-003.

ART-003 does not replace ART-001. ART-001 continues to govern main character production requirements. ART-003 establishes the protected visual soul and memory standard for Alamat's art direction.

## Purpose

The Alamat Visual Bible establishes the artistic identity of *Alamat ni Jay Jay*.

It exists to ensure that every piece of concept art, environment, character, animation, UI element, cinematic, illustration, and marketing asset feels like it belongs to the same world.

Technology, software, and artists may change.

The artistic soul of Alamat must not.

## The Emotional Goal

Players should feel like they are stepping into memories rather than simply exploring locations.

The world should inspire wonder through everyday life instead of spectacle.

Beauty comes from people, relationships, craftsmanship, and nature.

Adventure begins because home is worth leaving.

## The Visual Philosophy

- Home Before Adventure.
- Characters Before Architecture.
- Nature Before Civilization.
- Wonder Before Spectacle.
- Warmth Before Grandeur.
- Life Before Combat.
- Stories Before Landmarks.
- Hope Before Darkness.

Every location should feel worthy of being drawn inside Jay Jay's sketchbook.

## Environment Design Principles

Every location should answer an emotional question before serving a gameplay purpose.

Examples:

| Location Type | Emotional Question |
| --- | --- |
| Home | Would I want to live here? |
| Forest | What secrets are waiting to be discovered? |
| Mountain | How small do I feel? |
| Temple | Who built this, and why? |
| Ruins | What has been forgotten? |

No location exists simply because the game needs another map.

## Architectural Language

Architecture reflects community rather than wealth.

Buildings are handcrafted.

Natural materials dominate.

Wood carries visible grain.

Stone shows age.

Roofs reveal individual craftsmanship.

Nothing appears mass-produced.

Homes should feel lived in.

Imperfection is beauty.

## Nature

Nature is alive.

Wind moves through trees.

Leaves fall naturally.

Birds perch on rooftops.

Water reflects changing skies.

Flowers grow where people walk.

Nature should never feel like background decoration.

## The World Feels Lived In

People have routines.

Children play.

Neighbors greet one another.

Laundry dries in the breeze.

Meals are prepared.

Tools remain where they were last used.

Homes reveal personalities.

Objects tell stories without dialogue.

## Color Philosophy

### Early Game

- Warm golds.
- Soft greens.
- Ocean blues.
- Natural woods.
- Earth tones.
- Comfort.

### Mid Game

- Greater contrast.
- Broader color variety.
- Wonder expands.

### Late Game

Color reflects emotional themes rather than simply becoming darker.

Hope should always remain visible.

## Lighting

Natural lighting first.

Sunrise represents possibility.

Sunset represents reflection.

Rain represents quiet.

Night represents peace before fear.

Lighting should guide emotion before gameplay.

## Character Design

Characters should feel approachable.

Faces communicate kindness before power.

Silhouettes should be memorable.

Clothing reflects occupation and personality.

Equipment should feel earned rather than decorative.

Nobody dresses to impress the player.

## The Sketchbook Test

Every environment should pass one question:

Would Jay Jay stop long enough to sketch this?

If the answer is no, continue iterating.

## The Memory Test

Twenty years after finishing Alamat, what does the player remember?

If the answer is only "a dungeon" or "a boss fight," the design has failed.

If the answer is "a place," "a person," "a conversation," "a sunrise," "a meal," "a tree," or "going home," then the design has succeeded.

## Final Doctrine

Players may forget mechanics.

Players may forget numbers.

Players rarely forget how a place made them feel.

Every asset created for *Alamat ni Jay Jay* should strive to become a memory.
