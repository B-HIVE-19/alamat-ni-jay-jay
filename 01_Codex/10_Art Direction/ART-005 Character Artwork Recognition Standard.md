## Canon Bible Authority Note

The top-level `../../99_Canon/Character Art Bible.md` is the current highest-authority character art standard.

ART-005 remains a protected supporting art-direction source, but future character artwork must follow the Canon Bible's Character Art Bible v2.0 when there is any difference in emphasis.

## Purpose

This standard guides official JRPG character artwork for *Alamat ni Jay Jay*.

The project creates fantasy interpretations of real people.

The responsibility of character art is to preserve the emotional identity of each person while presenting them as if they belong inside a timeless JRPG world.

This is not anime fan art.

This is not realism.

This is not generic JRPG concept art.

## Relationship to ART-004

ART-004, Character Representation Doctrine, defines how literal a character's resemblance may be based on narrative role.

ART-005 defines how artists preserve recognition inside the applicable ART-004 tier.

ART-005 does not override ART-004.

For Tier I characters, including Jay Jay, Kalika, Kuya, and Mark, recognition must preserve emotional truth, key identity signals, personality, expression, silhouette, and family memory while avoiding direct portrait recreation unless explicitly approved by the Creative Director.

For Tier II and Tier III characters, closer literal resemblance may be used when ART-004 allows it.

The guiding ratio should always be interpreted within the approved ART-004 tier:

- Preserve recognizable emotional and visual identity first.
- Use fantasy interpretation second.
- Never use fantasy elements to erase the person.

## Primary Objective

Every illustration should cause someone who personally knew the real individual to immediately smile and say:

"That's them."

If the artwork is technically beautiful but the real person is no longer recognizable, the artwork has failed.

Emotional recognition is the highest priority.

## The Jay Jay Standard

Jay Jay is the artistic benchmark for the entire franchise.

Every future character must feel like they were illustrated by the same art director.

Ask:

"Would this character naturally belong standing beside Jay Jay?"

If the answer is no, redesign.

## The Recognition Ratio

Within the applicable ART-004 representation tier, characters should remain faithful to the real person's recognizable identity before they become fantasy designs.

Use the 80 / 20 rule as a production reminder:

- 80 percent faithful to the person's recognizable identity.
- 20 percent fantasy interpretation.

Never reverse this ratio.

For Tier I characters, this does not mean direct portrait recreation. It means emotional recognizability, personality, body language, expression, and key identity signals should lead the design.

Fantasy exists to celebrate the person, not replace them.

## Facial Design

Every face begins from the provided reference photos when reference photos are approved for use.

Preserve:

- Face shape.
- Cheeks.
- Jawline.
- Nose.
- Eyes.
- Smile.
- Eyebrows.
- Expressions.
- Hair, including baldness when relevant.

Do not replace facial identity with a generic anime face.

## Eyes

Eyes may be anime-inspired.

They should not be oversized.

They should not be doll-like.

Eyes should communicate personality first.

## Nose

Noses should feel natural and painterly.

Never reduce a person's nose to a tiny generic anime triangle.

## Smile

The smile is one of the strongest identifiers.

Never replace someone's real smile with a generic anime smile.

## Body Types

Every body should remain faithful to the real person or to the approved character doctrine.

Do not idealize.

Do not create superhero bodies unless that character's canon specifically requires it.

Production examples:

- Jay Jay: soft, round, warm, friendly.
- Kuya Ric: broad shoulders, strong, average build, not a bodybuilder.
- Kalika: naturally curvy, comfortable, never thin simply because she is a heroine.

## Clothing

Clothing should be modern fantasy.

Every outfit should begin from clothing the real person would realistically wear or from the approved character doctrine.

Then layer fantasy elements over it.

Fantasy should never erase identity.

## Materials

Preferred materials include:

- Leather.
- Cloth.
- Wood.
- Metal.
- Fabric.

Everything should feel handcrafted.

Avoid:

- Sci-fi armor.
- Plastic materials.
- Tactical military aesthetics unless essential to the character.

## Paint Style

Character artwork should use warm painterly illustration, soft rendering, and rich lighting.

Avoid:

- Hyperrealism.
- Plastic skin.
- Cel shading.
- Comic book ink.
- Overly exaggerated anime rendering.

Influence may be drawn from classic and modern JRPG visual language, including *Final Fantasy*, *Ni no Kuni*, *Dragon Quest*, *Granblue Fantasy*, and Studio Ghibli background warmth, but these references should blend into a unique Alamat identity.

## Color

Use:

- Warm lighting.
- Natural skin tones.
- Rich purples.
- Gold accents.
- Earth tones.

Fantasy should feel lived in.

## Expression Sheets

Expressions should capture recognizable personality.

Avoid generic emotional labels when a character-specific expression is more accurate.

Instead of only:

- Happy.
- Sad.
- Angry.

Use expressions unique to the character, such as:

- Fake Serious.
- The Face.
- Big Laugh.
- Protective.
- Thinking.
- Wonder.
- Embarrassed.
- Curious.
- Goofy Smile.
- Proud.

Every expression should immediately remind loved ones of that person.

## Companions

Companions are not pets.

They are family.

Every companion should have:

- Unique personality.
- Unique silhouette.
- Unique emotional role.

Examples include Bee 10, Siga, and Pandi.

Treat companions as playable party members or emotionally equal character presences when their role supports it.

## Family Heraldry

Family heraldry may appear naturally in character design.

Established heraldry includes:

- House Moralde: Eagle Crest.
- House Bernaldo: Bee Crest.

Crests may appear naturally on:

- Clothing.
- Jewelry.
- Weapons.
- Armor.
- Satchels.
- Companion gear.
- Banners.

Crests should never be oversized.

Crests should never be purely decorative.

## Fantasy Rule

Fantasy enhances identity.

Fantasy never replaces identity.

Weapons should reflect personality.

Armor should reflect lifestyle.

Magic should reflect emotion.

## World Feel

The world should feel hopeful, handcrafted, magical, and emotionally warm.

Every character should feel like they belong in the same universe.

## Five Second Test

Show the artwork to someone who knew the real person for only five seconds.

If they immediately know who it is, the artwork is succeeding.

If they hesitate, redesign.

For Tier I characters, the test may be applied to emotional recognition and identity signals rather than exact portrait likeness.

## Smile Test

Every finished illustration should make someone who loved that person smile.

If it does not, continue refining.

## Final Checklist

Before approving official character artwork, confirm:

- The character preserves recognizable identity within the approved ART-004 tier.
- The face preserves the strongest approved identity signals.
- The personality is visible before the class role.
- The character fits naturally beside Jay Jay.
- Fantasy enhances identity rather than replacing it.
- The artwork uses warm painterly JRPG style.
- House crests, if used, are integrated naturally.
- Companions support the character emotionally.
- Family or loved ones would recognize the emotional truth.

Do not create the coolest character.

Create the most recognizable fantasy version of the person that the applicable ART-004 tier allows.

The highest compliment is not:

"This looks amazing."

The highest compliment is:

"That's them."

## Production Use

Use ART-005 when creating or reviewing:

- Character concept art.
- Character locks.
- Portrait sheets.
- Expression sheets.
- Body and silhouette studies.
- Costume designs.
- Companion designs.
- Heraldry integration.
- Marketing character art.
- Playable character lineup art.
- Family/supporting cast likeness work.
- Cameo artwork.
