## Purpose

The Gameplay Bible defines how the playable systems of *Alamat ni Jay Jay* work together to create adventure, curiosity, danger, warmth, and memorable JRPG discovery.

Gameplay should never become a detached feature checklist. Combat, equipment, magic, inventory, crafting, and party systems must support the player's emotional journey through a living world.

## Gameplay Pillars

1. Exploration creates desire.
2. Danger gives travel meaning.
3. Combat expresses character and place.
4. Equipment carries memory, craft, and identity.
5. Magic belongs to the world, not a generic spell list.
6. Inventory should support adventure rhythm without becoming clutter.
7. Crafting should connect to resources, people, culture, and discovery.
8. Party systems should express relationships, trust, humor, loyalty, and growth.

## Completion Gate

Before a gameplay system is considered complete, ask:

- Would this make 12-year-old Jeff excited to keep playing?
- Would this help someone remember *Alamat ni Jay Jay* twenty years from now?
- Does this honor the emotional reason the project exists?

If any answer is no, keep designing.
