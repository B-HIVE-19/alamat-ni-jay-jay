## Purpose

The Folklore Bible governs how *Alamat ni Jay Jay* adapts Filipino folklore inspiration into an original fantasy world with respect, wonder, mystery, and internal consistency.

This document does not replace real folklore research. It defines how folklore-inspired material enters Alamat without becoming a generic monster list, trivia catalog, or direct recreation of historical belief.

## Design Standard

Every folklore element should answer three questions:

1. What truth does this legend preserve?
2. How does this belief affect daily life, travel, architecture, rituals, humor, fear, or law?
3. Would discovering this make 12-year-old Jeff excited to keep exploring?

If the answer to the third question is no, the idea needs more wonder, more specificity, or more emotional purpose.

## Adaptation Rules

- Respect Filipino inspiration through thoughtful research and careful transformation.
- Treat folklore as living memory, not decoration.
- Preserve regional disagreement where it creates depth.
- Allow humor, but never mock the culture or emotional roots of a belief.
- Do not explain away every mystery.
- Do not make every spirit or creature hostile.
- Connect folklore to gameplay through exploration, choices, dungeons, quests, music, art, and environmental storytelling.

## Canon Rule

Folklore-inspired material remains draft until its specific Alamat interpretation is reviewed for cultural respect, world consistency, emotional purpose, and gameplay value.
