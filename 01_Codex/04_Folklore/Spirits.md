## Purpose

This document tracks spirits and presences in Alamat: beings that are not primarily creatures, enemies, gods, or NPCs, but still shape how people live, travel, build, remember, and behave.

## Spirit Design Philosophy

Spirits should feel tied to place, memory, obligation, and atmosphere. A spirit encounter should not automatically mean combat. It may be a warning, blessing, echo, bargain, puzzle, haunting, joke, or quiet sign that the world is older than the party understands.

## Spirit Categories

| Category | Description | Gameplay Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Place Spirits | Bound to springs, trees, bridges, reefs, houses, terraces, caves, or ruins. | Exploration rules, offerings, hidden routes, environmental changes. |
| Household Presences | Tied to homes, hearths, family memory, tools, or thresholds. | Village quests, etiquette, humor, emotional scenes. |
| Ancestral Echoes | Fragments of memory connected to families, ruins, songs, or heirlooms. | Story revelations, optional lore, character growth. |
| Boundary Presences | Associated with crossroads, doors, paths, bridges, shorelines, and old gates. | Navigation puzzles, warnings, dungeon transitions. |
| Wounded Presences | Spirits distorted by neglect, betrayal, disaster, or broken agreements. | Restoration quests, boss foreshadowing, moral choices. |
