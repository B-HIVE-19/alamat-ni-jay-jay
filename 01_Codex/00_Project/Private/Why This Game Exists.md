## Purpose

This private Codex note exists to preserve the personal reason *Alamat ni Jay Jay* is being made.

Public-facing documents explain what the game is. This document is for why it matters.

## Tribute Foundation

This project exists as a tribute.

Jay Jay, the protagonist of *Alamat ni Jay Jay*, is inspired by Jeffrey Moralde's late brother.

This is not a biography.

This is not an attempt to recreate his life.

It is an original work of fiction inspired by the qualities Jeffrey loved most about him.

In this world, Jay Jay becomes the hero he always dreamed of being.

The supporting cast is inspired by real people who have influenced Jeffrey's life, but they are original fictional characters.

## Creative Heart

*Alamat ni Jay Jay* exists to create the feeling of a grand adventure: wonder, curiosity, danger, humor, family, friendship, loyalty, and the sense that the world is much older than the player can see at first.

The game should carry the emotional memory of classic JRPG exploration while becoming something original, personal, and culturally grounded.

The emotional authenticity of Alamat is more important than spectacle.

This project should be approached with the respect given to a lifelong creative legacy.

## Player Memory Target

We are not building just another RPG.

We are building the game that 12-year-old Jeff would have played over an entire weekend, finished with a smile, and remembered for the next thirty years.

Every creative decision should move the project closer to that experience.

## Personal Foundation

Approved private foundation:

- The main character, Jay Jay, is inspired by Jeffrey Moralde's late brother.
- The game is not biographical and should not attempt to recreate real life.
- Jay Jay is an original fictional hero inspired by loved qualities, memory, and spirit.
- Supporting cast members may be inspired by real people who influenced Jeffrey's life, but they remain original fictional characters.

## Spirit Test

Whenever making creative decisions, ask:

> Does this honor the spirit of the project?

If not, rethink the idea.

## What This Game Should Protect

- The feeling of leaving home and discovering a larger world.
- The warmth of companions who feel like real people.
- The mystery of legends that contain truth.
- The danger and satisfaction of first-person dungeon exploration.
- Filipino-inspired fantasy treated with care, specificity, and respect.
- Humor and heart even when the story becomes dark.
- Jay Jay as an original hero inspired by love, memory, and tribute.
- The emotional authenticity of the project over spectacle.

## What This Game Should Never Become

- A generic fantasy RPG with Filipino surface decoration.
- A nostalgia project that only imitates older games.
- A dungeon crawler where locations exist only as maps.
- A story where companions exist only to serve Jay Jay.
- A project that sacrifices wonder for convenience.
- A biography or direct recreation of real lives.
- A spectacle-first project that loses the tribute at its heart.

## Open Reflection Questions

- What personal feeling should the finished game preserve?
- What should players understand about Jay Jay by the end?
- What should this game say about family, friendship, and loyalty?
- What would make this project feel honest rather than merely impressive?
- What qualities of Jeffrey's brother should Jay Jay honor through fiction?
- Would 12-year-old Jeff have wanted to keep exploring this world for one more hour?
