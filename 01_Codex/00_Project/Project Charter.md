## Purpose

This charter establishes the foundation for *Alamat ni Jay Jay* during pre-production. It defines the project identity, creative mandate, audience, emotional target, design goals, and canon rules.

This document contains no story spoilers. It is intended as a stable reference for all future development.

## Project Name

*Alamat ni Jay Jay*

## Creator

Jeffrey Moralde

## Genre

Original classic JRPG with first-person dungeon exploration and turn-based battles.

## Vision

*Alamat ni Jay Jay* is an original commercial-quality JRPG about embarking on a grand adventure through a Filipino-inspired fantasy island world filled with mystery, danger, humor, family, friendship, loyalty, ancient history, living folklore, and meaningful first-person dungeon exploration.

The project preserves the emotional feeling of classic JRPG adventure while building a completely original world, cast, mythology, and identity.

At its heart, the project exists as a tribute. Jay Jay is an original fictional hero inspired by the qualities Jeffrey Moralde loved most about his late brother. This is not a biography and not an attempt to recreate real life.

## Mission

Create a heartfelt, memorable JRPG that delivers wonder, curiosity, adventure, emotional attachment, and discovery from beginning to end.

The game should feel like an experience a player could finish with a smile and remember decades later.

## Core Philosophy

Adventure comes first. Curiosity is always rewarded. Every dungeon tells a story. Every legend contains truth. Characters are remembered before combat systems.

The project must protect the emotional heart of Alamat above spectacle, trends, or convenience.

## Target Audience

Primary audience:

- Players who love classic JRPG adventures.
- Players drawn to exploration, party bonds, mystery, and memorable dungeons.
- Players interested in original fantasy worlds inspired by Filipino culture and folklore.

Secondary audience:

- Fans of retro-inspired RPGs seeking modern emotional depth.
- Players who value character-driven adventure and handcrafted worldbuilding.
- Players who enjoy first-person dungeon exploration with purpose, atmosphere, and discovery.

## Primary Inspirations

Primary emotional inspiration:

- The feeling of adventure, mystery, danger, and discovery found in classic JRPGs such as the original *Phantasy Star*.

Important boundaries:

- The project does not copy *Phantasy Star* story, characters, world, maps, factions, terminology, or specific content.
- The goal is to recreate an emotional experience, not imitate an existing intellectual property.

World and identity inspirations:

- Filipino folklore.
- Philippine-inspired geography, landscapes, island life, architecture, traditions, values, oral history, and ancient-civilization mystery.

This is not historical Philippines. It is an original fantasy world.

## Emotional Experience

The player should feel:

- Wonder at entering a world older than the story.
- Curiosity about legends, ruins, islands, companions, and secrets.
- Risk when exploring dangerous spaces.
- Warmth from humor, friendship, family, loyalty, and party bonds.
- Satisfaction from discovering truths hidden inside myths.
- Attachment to companions as people, not functions.
- Triumph after surviving meaningful dungeons and returning changed.

## Design Goals

1. Build an original fantasy world that feels centuries old.
2. Make every major location feel lived-in, historical, and memorable.
3. Design first-person dungeons that tell stories through space.
4. Reward curiosity with secrets, lore, shortcuts, treasure, character insight, and wonder.
5. Develop characters with independent lives, flaws, humor, goals, fears, and emotional growth.
6. Make dialogue recognizable by voice alone.
7. Treat Filipino inspiration with respect, authenticity, specificity, and research.
8. Protect visual consistency and avoid generic fantasy art.
9. Preserve the emotional feeling of classic JRPG adventure while building an original identity.
10. Ensure every design decision supports immersion.

## Development Principles

- The Alamat Codex is the single source of truth.
- Preserve approved canon unless Jeffrey Moralde explicitly changes it.
- Build upon existing knowledge rather than replacing it.
- Identify contradictions before changing lore.
- Keep draft material clearly marked until approved.
- Prefer cohesive design over isolated ideas.
- Evaluate new concepts against the Vision, Alamat Doctrine, Studio Mandate, and Codex Governance.
- Protect emotional authenticity over spectacle.
- Protect Alamat's identity over trends.

## Creative Rules

All creative decisions must follow the Alamat Doctrine:

1. Adventure comes first.
2. Curiosity is always rewarded.
3. Every dungeon tells a story.
4. Every legend contains truth.
5. Characters are remembered before combat systems.
6. Humor balances darkness.
7. Every companion has their own life.
8. Filipino inspiration should be respectful, authentic, and thoughtfully researched.
9. Family, friendship, courage, and loyalty are recurring themes.
10. Wonder should exist from beginning to end.
11. Players should become emotionally attached to the party.
12. Every major location has history.
13. Every design decision should support immersion.
14. Never sacrifice identity for trends.
15. Protect the emotional heart of Alamat above everything else.

## Success Criteria

The project succeeds when:

- The game feels like a grand adventure rather than a collection of systems.
- Players remember the party as people.
- Players want to explore because curiosity consistently pays off.
- Dungeons are remembered by identity, history, landmarks, secrets, and emotional experience.
- The world feels larger and older than the main story.
- Filipino inspiration feels respectful, thoughtful, and original.
- Humor and heart remain present even when the story becomes dark.
- The player feels wonder from beginning to end.
- The finished game honors the spirit of the project.
- The experience moves toward the game 12-year-old Jeff would have played over a weekend, finished with a smile, and remembered for thirty years.

## Canon Rules

1. Approved canon may not be overwritten without explicit permission from Jeffrey Moralde.
2. Private canon must be handled with additional care and respect.
3. Jay Jay is inspired by Jeffrey Moralde's late brother, but he is an original fictional character.
4. *Alamat ni Jay Jay* is a tribute, not a biography.
5. Supporting cast members may be inspired by real people, but they are original fictional characters.
6. New ideas must be checked against established Vision, Doctrine, and existing Codex documents before expansion.
7. Conflicts must be identified before changes are made.
8. Deprecated material should be preserved for history.
9. Major design decisions must be recorded in the Decision Log.
10. The Codex must remain professional, organized, expandable, and internally consistent.

## Required Creative Tests

Before approving major work, ask:

- Does this create wonder, curiosity, and adventure?
- Does this honor the spirit of the project?
- Does this move us closer to the game 12-year-old Jeff would remember for decades?
- Does this protect the emotional heart of Alamat?
- Does this strengthen Alamat's identity as an original Filipino-inspired fantasy JRPG?
