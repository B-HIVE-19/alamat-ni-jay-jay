## Purpose

The Alamat Doctrine governs every creative decision for *Alamat ni Jay Jay*. It is the top-level decision filter for story, worldbuilding, dungeon design, character development, combat, quests, art, music, UI, technical direction, and production tradeoffs.

Never sacrifice these principles for convenience.

These principles are permanent unless officially revised through the Decision Log and Codex Governance process.

## North-Star Test

When making any creative decision, ask:

> Will this make someone feel the same sense of wonder, curiosity, and adventure that players experienced exploring the world and first-person dungeons of the original *Phantasy Star*?

If the answer is no, rethink the idea.

The objective is not to imitate *Phantasy Star*. The objective is to create a new RPG that gives today's players that same unforgettable feeling of embarking on a grand adventure.

## Spirit Test

When making any creative decision, also ask:

> Does this honor the spirit of the project?

If the answer is no, rethink the idea.

## Player Memory Test

When evaluating a major creative decision, ask:

> Does this move us closer to the game that 12-year-old Jeff would have played over an entire weekend, finished with a smile, and remembered for the next thirty years?

If the answer is no, rethink the idea.

## Doctrine

1. Adventure comes first.
2. Curiosity is always rewarded.
3. Every dungeon tells a story.
4. Every legend contains truth.
5. Characters are remembered before combat systems.
6. Humor balances darkness.
7. Every companion has their own life.
8. Filipino inspiration should be respectful, authentic, and thoughtfully researched.
9. Family, friendship, courage, and loyalty are recurring themes.
10. Wonder should exist from beginning to end.
11. Players should become emotionally attached to the party.
12. Every major location has history.
13. Every design decision should support immersion.
14. Never sacrifice identity for trends.
15. Protect the emotional heart of Alamat above everything else.

## Design Philosophy

Design must serve adventure, immersion, and emotional memory before novelty or convenience.

Why this exists:
*Alamat ni Jay Jay* is not a feature checklist. Systems, progression, combat, UI, quests, and rewards must work together to make the player feel they are on a grand journey through a living world. A mechanic is only successful if it strengthens curiosity, attachment, danger, wonder, or the identity of Alamat.

Design principles:

- Prefer meaningful choices over mechanical clutter.
- Reward attention, exploration, and emotional investment.
- Make systems readable without making the world feel artificial.
- Let mechanics express character, culture, place, and story.
- Reject trends that weaken the project's identity.

## Story Philosophy

Story should create an emotional journey, not simply move the player from event to event.

Why this exists:
The project is a tribute and a lifelong creative legacy. Its story must honor that spirit through sincerity, warmth, courage, humor, grief, loyalty, and wonder. Plot exists to reveal people, places, choices, and truths hidden inside legends.

Story principles:

- Preserve emotional authenticity over spectacle.
- Avoid story beats that exist only because RPGs usually have them.
- Let legends contain truth, even when distorted.
- Create memorable moments rather than merely progressing the plot.
- Keep the game spoiler-aware in foundational documents.

## Character Philosophy

Characters must be remembered as people before they are understood as party members, combat roles, rivals, mentors, or quest functions.

Why this exists:
Players should become emotionally attached to the party. Every major character needs an inner life, history, flaws, humor, fears, relationships, and a future that could exist without Jay Jay. Combat roles are secondary because players remember companions for who they are, not only what they do in battle.

Character principles:

- Build personality before mechanics.
- Give every companion their own life.
- Let relationships evolve through trust, conflict, teasing, comfort, and time.
- Make emotional growth believable and earned.
- Protect Jay Jay as an original fictional hero created in tribute, not a biographical recreation.

## Dungeon Philosophy

Every dungeon must tell a story.

Why this exists:
First-person dungeons are central to the game's identity. They should evoke wonder, curiosity, risk, isolation, discovery, and triumph. A dungeon that exists only as a gameplay obstacle weakens the heart of the project.

Dungeon principles:

- Give every dungeon history, legend, purpose, ecology, architecture, and secrets.
- Make the player learn the space, not merely pass through it.
- Use landmarks, loops, hidden areas, puzzles, and boss encounters to express identity.
- Reward curiosity with discovery, not only treasure.
- Ensure the dungeon remains memorable even without enemies.

## Exploration Philosophy

Exploration should feel dangerous, inviting, and worth the risk.

Why this exists:
The project aims to recreate the emotional experience of classic JRPG adventure: the feeling that every path might reveal something strange, useful, frightening, funny, or beautiful. Curiosity must be rewarded because exploration is one of the primary ways players form attachment to the world.

Exploration principles:

- Make optional paths meaningful.
- Let the world suggest secrets without overexplaining them.
- Balance safety, risk, retreat, and reward.
- Use geography, weather, sound, architecture, and local belief to guide curiosity.
- Make discovery feel earned.

## World Building Philosophy

The world must feel like it existed long before Jay Jay's journey and will continue after it.

Why this exists:
Alamat cannot feel like a set of levels arranged for the player. It must feel like a living fantasy world shaped by Filipino-inspired folklore, landscapes, architecture, values, oral history, island life, ancient civilizations, trade, climate, and memory. Every major place needs history because history creates belief.

World building principles:

- Never create generic fantasy locations.
- Build geography, politics, economy, religion, architecture, ecology, and language as connected systems.
- Treat Filipino inspiration with respect, authenticity, and thoughtful research.
- Keep the world original, not historical Philippines.
- Make every legend serve a purpose in the living world.

## Dialogue Philosophy

Dialogue should reveal personality before information.

Why this exists:
Players should recognize major characters by voice alone. Dialogue is not only a delivery system for lore; it is how characters interrupt, disagree, laugh, tease, argue, comfort, hide, and grow. Natural humor comes from personality, not from detachable jokes.

Dialogue principles:

- Avoid exposition whenever possible.
- Let conflict, discovery, misunderstanding, and bias carry information.
- Give every major character a distinct rhythm, vocabulary, humor style, and emotional tell.
- Let relationships change how characters speak to each other.
- Use silence and evasion as meaningful dialogue tools.

## Art Philosophy

Visual design must protect the identity of Alamat.

Why this exists:
The game should feel unmistakably Filipino-inspired while remaining original. Art must communicate personality, region, climate, history, folklore, and emotional tone without falling into generic fantasy or surface-level decoration.

Art principles:

- Protect visual consistency across characters, locations, dungeons, creatures, UI, props, and animation.
- Require silhouette recognition for major characters and creatures.
- Tie palettes, clothing, materials, and architecture to world logic.
- Make every village, dungeon, and creature reinforce the world of Alamat.
- Transform inspiration thoughtfully rather than copying historical references directly.

## Music Philosophy

Music should carry memory, place, danger, warmth, and wonder.

Why this exists:
Classic JRPGs are remembered not only through story and play, but through melodies that make places and companions live in memory. Alamat's music should support emotional continuity across exploration, battle, villages, dungeons, grief, humor, triumph, and discovery.

Music principles:

- Give major places and characters memorable musical identities.
- Let instrumentation support Filipino-inspired texture without becoming pastiche.
- Use motifs to connect memory, family, friendship, legends, and adventure.
- Make dungeon music support isolation, danger, curiosity, and triumph.
- Ensure music strengthens emotional authenticity rather than overpowering it.

## Player Experience Philosophy

The player should finish the game with affection, wonder, and memory.

Why this exists:
The player memory target is central: this should feel like the game 12-year-old Jeff would have played over a weekend, finished with a smile, and remembered for thirty years. The player's experience is the final measure of whether the project honored its spirit.

Player experience principles:

- The journey should feel like a grand adventure from beginning to end.
- Players should want to explore because the world keeps answering curiosity with meaning.
- Players should become attached to the party.
- Players should remember dungeons by identity, not only difficulty.
- Players should feel that Alamat had a heart, not just content.

## Creative Decision Filter

Before approving a major feature, scene, dungeon, location, character, quest, or system, check it against these questions:

| Doctrine | Approval Question |
| --- | --- |
| Adventure comes first. | Does this make the journey feel more alive, uncertain, and worth taking? |
| Curiosity is always rewarded. | Does the player gain something meaningful by looking closer, asking why, or going off the obvious path? |
| Every dungeon tells a story. | Does this dungeon communicate history, purpose, danger, and meaning through exploration? |
| Every legend contains truth. | Does the myth reveal, distort, hide, or preserve something real? |
| Characters are remembered before combat systems. | Would players remember this person before they remember their role or mechanics? |
| Humor balances darkness. | Is there room for warmth, awkwardness, wit, or human relief without breaking the stakes? |
| Every companion has their own life. | Would this person still have goals, fears, relationships, duties, and a future without Jay Jay? |
| Filipino inspiration should be respectful, authentic, and thoughtfully researched. | Is the inspiration specific, respectful, researched, and transformed into original fantasy rather than reduced to surface symbols? |
| Family, friendship, courage, and loyalty are recurring themes. | Does the work deepen bonds, obligations, trust, bravery, betrayal, or chosen connection? |
| Wonder should exist from beginning to end. | Does this create awe, surprise, beauty, mystery, or emotional discovery? |
| Players should become emotionally attached to the party. | Would players miss these companions after the game ends? |
| Every major location has history. | What happened here before the player arrived? |
| Every design decision should support immersion. | Does this make Alamat feel more believable, cohesive, and lived-in? |
| Never sacrifice identity for trends. | Is this choice true to Alamat rather than chasing a fashionable convention? |
| Protect the emotional heart of Alamat above everything else. | Does this preserve the project's warmth, wonder, adventure, cultural specificity, and personal meaning? |
| Spirit of the project. | Does this honor the tribute at the heart of Alamat without turning the game into biography? |
| Player memory target. | Does this help create a game that could be remembered with affection decades later? |

## Non-Negotiable Standard

Convenience is not a valid reason to flatten culture, remove history, reduce characters to roles, make dungeons generic, erase humor, turn exploration into simple route completion, chase trends, weaken immersion, compromise the emotional heart of Alamat, or disrespect the tribute foundation of the project.
