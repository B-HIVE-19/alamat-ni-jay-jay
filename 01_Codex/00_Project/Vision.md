## Core Idea

*Alamat ni Jay Jay* is an original classic JRPG about embarking on a grand adventure through a Filipino-inspired fantasy island world filled with mystery, danger, humor, family, friendship, loyalty, ancient history, living folklore, and first-person dungeon exploration.

The project should preserve the emotional feeling of classic JRPG adventure while building a completely original world, cast, mythology, and identity.

At its heart, the project exists as a tribute. Jay Jay is an original fictional hero inspired by the qualities Jeffrey loved most about his late brother. The supporting cast may be inspired by real people who influenced Jeffrey's life, but they remain original fictional characters.

## North-Star Question

When making any creative decision, ask:

> Will this make someone feel the same sense of wonder, curiosity, and adventure that players experienced exploring the world and first-person dungeons of the original *Phantasy Star*?

If the answer is no, rethink the idea.

The objective is not to imitate *Phantasy Star*. The objective is to create a new RPG that gives today's players that same unforgettable feeling of embarking on a grand adventure.

## Player Memory Target

We are not building just another RPG.

We are building the game that 12-year-old Jeff would have played over an entire weekend, finished with a smile, and remembered for the next thirty years.

Every creative decision should move the project closer to that experience.

## Spirit Question

When making any creative decision, also ask:

> Does this honor the spirit of the project?

If not, rethink the idea.

The emotional authenticity of Alamat is more important than spectacle. This project should be approached with the respect given to a lifelong creative legacy.

## Player Fantasy

The player fantasy is to step into a world that feels older and larger than the main story, where every island, dungeon, legend, village, ruin, and companion invites curiosity.

Players should feel that they are:

- Leaving home for something bigger than they understand.
- Entering places with real history and danger.
- Discovering truths hidden inside legends.
- Building bonds with companions who feel alive beyond their combat roles.
- Choosing to push deeper into mystery because wonder outweighs fear.

## Emotional Tone

The emotional tone should balance wonder, danger, warmth, humor, melancholy, and discovery.

The journey should feel adventurous first: mysterious enough to pull the player forward, dangerous enough to matter, funny enough to feel human, and heartfelt enough to be remembered after the game ends.
