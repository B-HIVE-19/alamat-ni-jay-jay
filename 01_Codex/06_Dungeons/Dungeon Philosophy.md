## Purpose

Dungeon Philosophy defines why first-person dungeons exist in *Alamat ni Jay Jay* and how they should feel.

The original *Phantasy Star* inspires the emotional experience of mystery, danger, isolation, discovery, and triumph. Alamat must not copy its layouts, story, or world.

## Core Principle

Every dungeon tells a story.

A dungeon is not a hallway between plot points. It is a place with purpose, history, architecture, ecology, secrets, music, danger, and memory.

## Player Experience

Players should feel:

- Curious before entering.
- Slightly unsafe while exploring.
- Rewarded for noticing details.
- Oriented by landmarks, sounds, and patterns.
- Nervous when supplies, light, or direction become uncertain.
- Triumphant when they understand the place rather than merely reach the exit.

## Design Tests

Before a dungeon is approved, ask:

1. Would this make 12-year-old Jeff excited to keep playing?
2. Would someone remember this dungeon twenty years from now?
3. Does this dungeon honor the emotional reason the project exists?
4. Could this dungeon exist without Jay Jay entering it?
5. Does curiosity reveal secrets, shortcuts, story, treasure, or emotional meaning?

If any answer is no, keep designing.
