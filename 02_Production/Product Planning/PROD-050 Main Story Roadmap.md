## Purpose

PROD-050 is the production roadmap for developing the main narrative of *Alamat ni Jay Jay*.

This document does not write dialogue, cutscenes, final lore, final chapter scripts, or final boss mechanics. It defines the shape of narrative production so the team can build story, dungeons, towns, emotional pacing, mysteries, character growth, and exploration in a coordinated way.

## Creative Philosophy

1. Adventure first.
2. Characters before mechanics.
3. Every dungeon tells a story.
4. Every legend has truth.
5. Wonder before spectacle.
6. Curiosity should change what the player understands.
7. Humor should survive danger.
8. Quiet moments are production requirements, not optional filler.
9. The party must feel like people the player would miss.
10. The ending must honor the emotional reason the project exists.

## Production Structure Overview

The current roadmap uses a four-act JRPG structure. Act names are production labels only.

| Act | Production Function | Player Feeling | Lock Level |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Act I | Home, first invitation, first danger, first companion bond, first proof that legends contain truth. | Warmth, curiosity, danger, first wonder. | Draft Framework |
| Act II | Wider world, party formation, regional exploration, early dungeon truths, conflicting local stories. | Expansion, attachment, risk, discovery. | Draft Framework |
| Act III | Consequences, deeper mysteries, party tests, major truth recontextualization, stronger danger. | Pressure, doubt, courage, emotional commitment. | Draft Framework |
| Act IV | Final approach, emotional resolution, truth faced, party payoffs, ending memory. | Triumph, grief, wonder, remembrance. | Draft Framework |

## Act I - The World Opens

### Narrative Function

Act I should make the player want to leave home without making home feel disposable. The familiar world should feel warm, funny, specific, and worth protecting before danger arrives.

### Major Story Beats

| Beat | Production Purpose | Lock Level |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Opening Daily Life | Establish Jay Jay's warmth, curiosity, relationships, and ordinary world. | Draft |
| Local Oddity | Introduce a small mystery that rewards curiosity without explaining the larger plot. | Draft |
| First Companion Contact | Introduce Kalika through agency, not rescue or exposition. | Draft |
| First Legend Fracture | Show that a familiar local legend contains some truth. | Draft |
| First Dangerous Route | Move beyond safety through exploration, not a forced corridor. | Draft |
| Dungeon 01 | First-person dungeon proves that places remember history. | Draft |
| First Bond Choice | Jay Jay and Kalika choose trust under pressure. | Draft |
| Departure Commitment | The adventure becomes chosen, not merely assigned. | Draft |

### Character Introductions

| Character | Introduction Goal | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Jay Jay | Show emotional center, kindness, wonder, and courage in ordinary behavior. | Treating him as blank protagonist or biography. |
| Kalika | Show wisdom through action, observation, restraint, and independent motive. | Making her exist only to guide Jay Jay. |
| Kuya Ric | Foreshadow or introduce as courage, protection, and grounded responsibility. | Reducing him to muscle or authority. |
| Mark | Foreshadow or introduce as curiosity, humor, and vulnerability. | Reducing him to comic relief. |

### Town Order

Working order:

1. Starting Village.
2. Nearby route or small settlement.
3. First village outside Jay Jay's familiar world.

Town names remain pending. Town order may change during story, world, and dungeon planning.

### Dungeon Order

Working order:

1. Dungeon 01 - First proof dungeon.

Dungeon 01 should be memorable for identity, atmosphere, and legend truth, not difficulty alone.

### Emotional Pacing

- Begin with warmth and humor.
- Add curiosity before danger.
- Let fear arrive through discovery rather than spectacle.
- End the act with chosen adventure and a larger question.

### Quiet Moments

Required quiet moments:

- Home-life observation before departure.
- First camp or rest moment after danger.
- A small shared laugh between Jay Jay and Kalika.
- A moment where the player sees what leaving home costs emotionally.

### Boss Encounter Purpose

Act I boss encounters should communicate that old stories are not harmless decorations. The first boss should be a narrative threshold, not just a test of combat.

Boss identity remains pending.

## Act II - Islands, Roads, and Companions

### Narrative Function

Act II expands the world and completes the early party shape. The player should realize that every island, village, route, and dungeon has its own history and that the party's relationships are becoming the emotional engine of the journey.

### Major Story Beats

| Beat | Production Purpose | Lock Level |
| --- | --- | --- |
| First Wider-World Hub | Introduce markets, boats, rumors, regional tensions, and travel choices. | Draft |
| Mark Introduction | Introduce curiosity as a force that opens doors and creates risk. | Draft |
| Route Mystery | Reward optional exploration with emotional or world truth. | Draft |
| Town With Contradictory Legend | Show that different communities remember the same event differently. | Draft |
| Dungeon 02 | A dungeon with a clear historical purpose and a different emotional identity from Dungeon 01. | Draft |
| Kuya Ric Introduction | Bring courage into the party through responsibility, not convenience. | Draft |
| Party Friction | Let the four heroes disagree without breaking trust. | Draft |
| First Major Regional Choice | Give the party a choice shaped by loyalty, risk, and incomplete information. | Draft |

### Character Growth Milestones

| Character | Milestone |
| --- | --- |
| Jay Jay | Learns that caring for everyone does not mean carrying every fear alone. |
| Kalika | Begins allowing others to see concern beneath control. |
| Kuya Ric | Shows that courage includes admitting uncertainty. |
| Mark | Discovers curiosity has consequences and responsibilities. |

### Town Order

Working order:

1. Market Port.
2. River Crossing Town.
3. Shrine Settlement or Mountain Hamlet.
4. Outer settlement tied to Dungeon 02.

Names, exact route logic, and political affiliations remain pending.

### Dungeon Order

Working order:

1. Dungeon 02 - Regional-history dungeon.
2. Optional or minor ruin route if later approved.

Dungeon 02 must contrast with Dungeon 01 in layout, atmosphere, legend, puzzle logic, and emotional purpose.

### Emotional Pacing

- Alternate travel wonder with interpersonal friction.
- Use marketplaces and boat travel to make the world feel larger.
- Let humor emerge from fatigue, uncertainty, and growing trust.
- End with the party emotionally stronger but more aware of danger.

### Quiet Moments

Required quiet moments:

- Boat ride conversation or silent horizon moment.
- Marketplace pause where the party sees ordinary life continuing.
- Campfire after disagreement.
- Mark noticing something beautiful before anyone else.
- Kuya Ric doing a protective action without announcing it.

### Boss Encounter Purpose

Act II bosses should reveal that regional legends and local politics are connected. Bosses may be creatures, corrupted guardians, human antagonists, or awakened consequences, but no boss should exist without a location story.

Boss identities remain pending.

## Act III - Truth Has Weight

### Narrative Function

Act III turns adventure into commitment. Discoveries should stop feeling distant or optional. The party begins to understand that legends preserve truth because truth was dangerous, painful, or politically inconvenient.

### Major Story Beats

| Beat | Production Purpose | Lock Level |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Recontextualized Legend | Reveal that an earlier story was incomplete, not false. | Draft |
| Party Trust Test | Force the group to act with incomplete trust or repair damaged trust. | Draft |
| Ancient-Civilization Threshold | Bring ancient history closer without fully explaining it. | Draft |
| Dungeon 03 | A deeper dungeon where environmental storytelling carries major emotional weight. | Pending Approval |
| Companion Focus Sequence | Give one companion a choice that is not about Jay Jay. | Draft |
| Cost of Curiosity | Exploration reveals something useful and emotionally difficult. | Draft |
| Major Mid-Late Revelation | Clarify stakes while preserving final mystery. | Draft |
| Irreversible Commitment | The party chooses to continue knowing the cost. | Draft |

### Character Growth Milestones

| Character | Milestone |
| --- | --- |
| Jay Jay | Chooses courage without needing certainty. |
| Kalika | Shares knowledge or fear she would previously have kept private. |
| Kuya Ric | Lets protection become trust, not control. |
| Mark | Turns curiosity into disciplined bravery. |

### Town Order

Working order:

1. Walled Hill City or regional authority settlement.
2. Archive, shrine, or ruin-adjacent community.
3. Settlement affected by earlier party choices.

Exact order remains flexible until AC-300 and AC-330 are expanded.

### Dungeon Order

Working order:

1. Dungeon 03 - Ancient-history threshold dungeon.
2. Dungeon 04 - Character-reflection or consequence dungeon.

Dungeon count, names, and final placement remain pending.

### Emotional Pacing

- Increase danger without losing wonder.
- Let revelations create silence, not only action.
- Use humor more sparingly but more meaningfully.
- Allow the player to feel the party choosing each other under pressure.

### Quiet Moments

Required quiet moments:

- Night watch where fear is admitted indirectly.
- A shared meal after a hard reveal.
- A location where the party does nothing but listen or observe.
- A companion privately changing how they speak to another companion.

### Boss Encounter Purpose

Act III boss encounters should test what the dungeon believes, remembers, or protects. The boss should embody the location's history or the cost of misunderstanding it.

Boss identities remain pending.

## Act IV - The Adventure Becomes Memory

### Narrative Function

Act IV resolves the emotional promise rather than merely escalating spectacle. The party should face final danger as people who have changed because of travel, friendship, humor, fear, loyalty, and discovery.

| Final Boss Sequence | Embody the central emotional and legendary conflict. | Pending Approval |
| Return / Transformation | Show what has changed without flattening mystery. | Draft |

| Character | Milestone |
| --- | --- |
| Jay Jay | Becomes heroic without losing tenderness. |
| Kalika | Lets wisdom include trust, grief, humor, and uncertainty. |
| Kuya Ric | Chooses courage that protects freedom, not only safety. |
| Mark | Becomes someone whose questions help others be brave. |

### Town Order

Working order:

1. Last major settlement.
2. Final route staging area.
3. Optional return to earlier location if emotionally earned.

Final location choices remain pending.

### Dungeon Order

Working order:

1. Final Dungeon - Legend-truth resolution dungeon.

The final dungeon must not be a generic evil castle. It must tell its own story and make prior dungeons feel connected in hindsight.

### Emotional Pacing

- Begin with resolve.
- Give space for tenderness before final danger.
- Use spectacle only after emotional meaning is clear.
- End with wonder, affection, and remembrance.

### Quiet Moments

Required quiet moments:

- Last campfire or last rest equivalent.
- A moment where the party recognizes how far they have come.
- A small piece of humor before final danger.
- A final image that honors Jay Jay's emotional center without turning him into a symbol.

### Boss Encounter Purpose

Final boss production must answer:

- What legend has been misunderstood?
- What truth is being faced?
- What emotional growth makes victory possible?
- How does the encounter honor adventure, friendship, courage, and loyalty?
- Why would this moment stay with the player twenty years later?

Final boss identity remains pending.

## Major Mysteries

Mysteries should be designed as trails of curiosity, not exposition locks.

| Mystery Type | Early Presence | Midgame Development | Late Payoff | Lock Level |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Local Legend Truth | Heard as ordinary story or warning. | Conflicting versions appear. | The truth is more human, older, or stranger than expected. | Draft |
| Ancient Civilization | Seen through ruins, tools, patterns, and taboos. | Scholars, locals, and factions disagree. | A meaningful truth reframes earlier locations. | Draft |
| Jay Jay's Heroism | Begins as desire, dream, or emotional possibility. | Tested through fear, loyalty, and choice. | Resolved through action that preserves tenderness. | Direction Approved |
| Party Trust | Begins through need and curiosity. | Friction, secrets, humor, and protection deepen it. | Companions act from earned trust. | Direction Approved |
| World Magic | Seen as ritual, rumor, danger, or everyday practice. | Magic appears socially complicated. | Final understanding remains wondrous, not fully technical. | Draft |

## Revelation Timing Rules

Revelations should be timed by emotional readiness, not information volume.

| Timing | Revelation Type | Rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Act I | Proof of Wonder | Reveal that legends can be true, but not why the whole world works. |
| Act II | Conflicting Truths | Reveal that communities remember differently for understandable reasons. |
| Act III | Cost and Consequence | Reveal what truth costs people, places, and relationships. |
| Act IV | Emotional Truth | Reveal enough to resolve the story while leaving the world larger than the plot. |

## Boss Encounter Roadmap

Bosses should be narrative punctuation marks, not only difficulty spikes.

| Encounter Tier | Narrative Purpose | Requirements |
| --- | --- | --- |
| First Boss | Crosses player from story into legend-truth. | Clear location identity, emotional surprise, readable danger. |
| Regional Boss | Embodies local history, ecological pressure, or political consequence. | Must connect to settlement and dungeon story. |
| Midgame Boss | Forces party to confront misunderstanding or cost. | Must create character or world consequence. |
| Late Boss | Tests changed party values. | Must reflect growth, loyalty, or hard truth. |
| Final Boss | Resolves the central emotional and legendary conflict. | Must honor adventure, friendship, courage, loyalty, and remembrance. |

## World Exploration Moment Requirements

Each act should include optional exploration that rewards curiosity.

Examples of acceptable rewards:

- A hidden route that explains how locals travel.
- A small shrine with a conflicting version of a legend.
- A quiet view that changes music or party banter.
- A market rumor that later gains meaning.
- A creature behavior clue.
- An old inscription that is not fully understood yet.
- A family story that reveals ordinary life.
- A shortcut that feels discovered, not granted.

Avoid rewards that are only currency, loot, or checklist completion.

## Ending Setup

The ending should be prepared throughout production by:

- Showing what Jay Jay values before danger.
- Making the party's trust visible through repeated small actions.
- Letting dungeons reveal that places carry memory.
- Letting legends be partially true before the final truth.
- Allowing humor and tenderness to continue late into the game.
- Making the final choice feel like the result of relationships, not destiny alone.

Ending specifics remain pending.

## Potential Sequel Hook Rules

Sequel hooks may exist only if they do not weaken the first game's emotional completion.

Allowed hook categories:

- A distant island or route still unexplored.
- An ancient question not fully answered.
- A song, map, or legend that points beyond the known realm.
- A surviving relationship or promise with future potential.
- A world consequence that suggests life continues after the ending.

Avoid:

- Ending on unresolved emotional debt.
- Withholding the main truth only to set up another game.
- Introducing a new villain after emotional resolution.
- Making the ending feel incomplete.
