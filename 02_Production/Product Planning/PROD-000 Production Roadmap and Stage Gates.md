## Purpose

PROD-000 is the master production roadmap for *Alamat ni Jay Jay*.

It organizes the entire project from concept through post-launch support into professional production stages and approval gates suitable for an independent studio pursuing commercial-quality JRPG development.

This document does not write story content, gameplay systems, lore reveals, dialogue, quest content, or final feature scope. It defines how the project moves from vision to release while protecting canon, emotional authenticity, and production discipline.

PROD-000 should answer:

- Where are we?
- What has been completed?
- What is currently in progress?
- What comes next?
- What must be approved before moving forward?

## Production Philosophy

Production exists to protect the creative heart of *Alamat ni Jay Jay*, not to flatten it.

The project must remain disciplined enough to finish and flexible enough to stay alive. Every stage gate should confirm that the team is moving closer to a playable, memorable, emotionally authentic JRPG.

The production roadmap follows the approved project philosophy:

1. Adventure First.
2. Characters Before Mechanics.
3. Every Dungeon Tells a Story.
4. Every Legend Has Truth.
5. Wonder Before Spectacle.
6. Humility Creates Heroes.
7. Respect the Player.

## Stage Gate Model

Each stage contains:

- Purpose.
- Objectives.
- Deliverables.
- Dependencies.
- Required Codex Entries.
- Approval Requirements.
- Risks.
- Exit Criteria.
- Creative Director Sign-off.

Stages may overlap in practical production, but approval gates should be treated as checkpoints. A later stage may begin exploratory work before an earlier stage is fully complete, but major production commitments should not advance until dependencies are reviewed.

## Narrative Director Notes

Disciplined production planning protects creative vision because it prevents the game from becoming a pile of disconnected good ideas.

*Alamat ni Jay Jay* depends on emotional continuity across characters, world, dungeons, music, art, combat, UI, and technical implementation. A stage-gate roadmap gives the team a way to ask, at every step: does this work move the game closer to the adventure we promised?

The goal is not bureaucracy. The goal is memory. A multi-year project needs a structure that remembers why each choice matters, so the finished game still carries the same wonder, humility, friendship, and hope that began it.
