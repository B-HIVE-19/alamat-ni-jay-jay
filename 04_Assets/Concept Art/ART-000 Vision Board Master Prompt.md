## Jay Jay Direction

Jay Jay should be:

- Youthful.
- Humble.
- Awkward.
- Curious.
- Friendly.
- Not overly heroic.
- Kind-eyed.
- Naturally smiling.
- Visually readable as an ordinary village boy who has no idea he will one day become a legend.

Proposed visual direction pending Character Lock approval:

- White Filipino-inspired embroidered tunic.
- Blue sash.
- Red scarf.
- Leather satchel.
- Simple wooden/golden heirloom staff.
- Simple boots.

Staff rule:

The staff must not look like an overpowered legendary weapon. It should feel personal, humble, inherited, practical, and emotionally meaningful.

## Kalika Direction

Kalika should read as:

- Scholar.
- Explorer.
- Elegant without being royal.
- Quietly confident.
- Observant rather than posed.
- Present in the world rather than staged for the viewer.

Proposed visual direction pending Character Lock approval:

- Book in hand.
- Outfit and posture should suggest study, travel, restraint, and independence.

## Kuya Ric Direction

Kuya Ric should read as:

- Large presence.
- Fearless.
- Protective.
- Confident.
- Warmly bold rather than grim.
- The person who would say "Tara" before walking directly into danger.

Visual expression:

- Confident grin.
- Protective stance.
- Strong silhouette that communicates courage without reducing him to a combat role.

## Mark Direction

Mark should read as:

- Energetic.
- Engineer.
- Relic specialist.
- Excited by discovery.
- Curious rather than serious.

Proposed visual direction pending Character Lock approval:

- Adjusting goggles.
- Surrounded by subtle relic technology.
- Visual energy should remain joyful, not chaotic clutter.

## World Direction

The world should immediately communicate:

"I want to explore this."

The setting is an original fantasy world inspired by the Philippines.

It must not be:

- Historical Philippines.
- A direct recreation of real locations.
- Generic fantasy with Filipino labels.

Use transformed inspiration from:

- Palawan-like limestone cliffs.
- Banaue-style terrace logic.
- Tropical coastlines.
- Volcanoes.
- Waterfalls.
- Ancient jungle ruins.
- Bamboo architecture.
- Stone temples.
- Coastal villages.
- Lush forests.
- Floating islands.
- Distant forgotten civilizations.

Everything should feel ancient, mysterious, hopeful, and alive.

## Lighting Direction

Lighting should use:

- Golden hour.
- Warm sunlight.
- Volumetric rays.
- Rich tropical color palette.
- Deep blue oceans.
- Emerald forests.
- Bright skies.
- Hopeful atmosphere.

Avoid grim fantasy lighting as the default.

## Style Direction

Target style:

- AAA JRPG key art.
- Studio Ghibli sense of wonder.
- Classic Square Enix promotional artwork energy.
- Modern AAA rendering quality.
- Hand-painted fantasy realism.
- Highly detailed.
- Cinematic composition.
- Natural facial expressions.
- Believable clothing materials.
- Subtle environmental storytelling.

Style rule:

The image should feel premium and commercial, but not hollow or trend-driven.

## Composition Direction

Preserve the core composition:

- Jay Jay remains the emotional center.
- Kalika stands beside him.
- Kuya Ric stands confidently behind the group.
- Mark is slightly forward, excited by discovery.
- The world stretches endlessly into the horizon.
- The characters feel like they are standing at the beginning of an unforgettable journey.

Composition should prioritize:

- Adventure before combat.
- Friendship before power.
- Wonder before spectacle.
- Hope before destiny.

## Logo Direction

1910 Creative Studios

Center:

ALAMAT NI JAY JAY

Logo goals:

- Premium fantasy franchise identity.
- Commercial JRPG readability.
- Iconic.
- Elegant.

Avoid:

- Overly ornate unreadable lettering.
- Generic fantasy type.
- Cultural motifs used as decoration without restraint.
- A logo that overwhelms the characters or world.

## Emotional Goal

The artwork should make viewers feel:

"I don't know this world..."

"...but I want to spend the next hundred hours exploring it."

The image should communicate:

- Adventure before combat.
- Friendship before power.
- Wonder before spectacle.
- Hope before destiny.

## Final Directive

This artwork is not simply promotional art.

It is the visual North Star for the entire *Alamat ni Jay Jay* project.

Every future environment, character render, cinematic, gameplay screen, UI element, marketing asset, and collector's edition should feel like it belongs to this world.

The final image should be of sufficient quality that it could serve as the official cover art for a commercial JRPG released by 1910 Creative Studios.

## Master Image Prompt

Use the attached artwork as the primary composition reference. Refine and elevate the composition into true AAA JRPG key art suitable for physical box art, Steam capsule artwork, PlayStation Store, Nintendo eShop, Xbox Store, Collector's Edition packaging, official website hero art, promotional posters, and long-term franchise identity.

Create official cover art for *ALAMAT NI JAY JAY*, a classic turn-based JRPG by 1910 Creative Studios about adventure, friendship, wonder, hope, humility, and discovery. Preserve the emotional composition: Jay Jay at the center as a humble, youthful, curious village boy with kind eyes and a natural smile; Kalika beside him as an elegant scholar-explorer observing the world; Kuya Ric behind the party with a fearless protective presence and confident grin; Mark slightly forward, energetic, excited, adjusting his goggles, surrounded by subtle relic technology.

The characters stand at the beginning of an unforgettable journey, overlooking an original Filipino-inspired fantasy world filled with tropical coastlines, deep blue oceans, emerald forests, volcanic silhouettes, waterfalls, ancient jungle ruins, bamboo architecture, stone temples, coastal villages, terrace-like landscapes, floating islands, and distant forgotten civilizations. The world should feel ancient, mysterious, hopeful, alive, and impossible to resist exploring.

Use golden-hour lighting, warm sunlight, volumetric rays, rich tropical colors, bright skies, and a hopeful atmosphere. Style should blend AAA JRPG key art, hand-painted fantasy realism, modern premium rendering quality, classic Square Enix promotional energy, and a Studio Ghibli-like sense of wonder. Facial expressions should feel natural and emotionally sincere. Clothing materials should feel believable and travel-worn. Environmental storytelling should be subtle but rich.

The logo should read ALAMAT NI JAY JAY as a premium commercial JRPG franchise mark, with 1910 Creative Studios above. Use Filipino-inspired geometric motifs sparingly and elegantly. The final image should communicate adventure before combat, friendship before power, wonder before spectacle, and hope before destiny.

## Negative Prompt / Avoidance Rules

Avoid:

- Grimdark fantasy.
- Generic medieval fantasy castles.
- Overpowered weapon posing.
- Hyper-aggressive hero expressions.
- Character redesigns that conflict with approved Character Locks.
- Treating Filipino inspiration as costume decoration.
- Real-world historical recreation.
- Real places copied directly.
- Excessive logo clutter.
- Overly busy relic technology.
- Making Jay Jay look like a chosen-warrior archetype.
- Making Kalika look royal instead of scholarly.
- Making Kuya Ric only a combat brute.
- Making Mark only comic relief.
- Spectacle that overwhelms friendship.
