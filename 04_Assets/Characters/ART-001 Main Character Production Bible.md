## Purpose

ART-001 is the master visual production document for the four main heroes of *Alamat ni Jay Jay*: Jay Jay, Kalika, Kuya Ric, and Mark.

This document gives artists, animators, sprite artists, portrait artists, UI artists, and marketing artists a shared production standard before final character art is approved. It does not redesign existing characters and does not lock unapproved details from the Character Locks.

ART-004, Character Representation Doctrine, governs how closely characters may visually resemble real-life inspirations. For the main-four tier, Jay Jay, Kalika, Kuya, and Mark should become fully realized original fantasy heroes rather than direct portrait recreations, while preserving emotional truth.

ART-005, Character Artwork Recognition Standard, governs how artists preserve recognizable identity, expression, body language, body type, costume identity, companion treatment, and family-recognition signals within the ART-004 tier boundaries.

## Canon Boundary

The current Character Locks for Jay Jay, Kalika, Kuya Ric, and Mark are pre-lock documents. Exact official appearance, height, age, color palette, clothing, hairstyle, accessories, weapons, and animation language remain pending unless specifically listed as locked canon in the relevant Character Lock.

ART-001 may define:

- Approval gates.
- Asset requirements.

- Rules for preventing off-model or off-canon art.

ART-001 may not define:

- Final heights.
- Final palettes.
- Final costumes.
- Final weapons.
- Final hairstyles.
- Final facial likenesses.
- Final body types.
- Final combat roles.

## Shared Visual Rules For The Main Party

1. The four heroes must read as a party from the same world, not four unrelated genre designs.
2. Each hero must remain identifiable by silhouette alone.
3. Personality must be readable before combat function.
4. Clothing must feel practical for island travel, rain, heat, villages, boats, ruins, and first-person dungeon danger.
5. Filipino inspiration must be specific, respectful, and transformed into original fantasy design.
6. Accessories must reveal habit, memory, duty, craft, humor, or relationship.
7. Palette decisions must support party readability, battle readability, emotional identity, and world logic.
8. No design may reduce a character inspired by a real person into caricature, costume shorthand, or literal biography.
9. Designs should feel warm enough for friendship and brave enough for danger.
10. The party should look like people players would miss after the ending.

## Concept Art Approval Workflow

1. Thumbnail exploration.
2. Silhouette review.
3. Personality read review.
4. Cultural and world-fit review.
5. Clothing and material logic review.
6. Palette review.
7. Expression and animation read review.
8. Sprite-scale readability review.
9. Portrait compatibility review.
10. Final Character Lock update.

No character design becomes official until the relevant Character Lock is updated and approved.

## Canon Visual Approval Tests

Before approving any main character visual, ask:

1. Does this preserve established canon?
2. Does this avoid redesigning without approval?
3. Does the character feel like a real person before a party role?
4. Is the silhouette recognizable?
5. Does the design feel original to Alamat?
6. Is Filipino inspiration respectful, specific, and transformed?
7. Does the design support friendship, family, loyalty, courage, wonder, and adventure?
8. Would 12-year-old Jeff want to keep playing after seeing this party?
9. Would players remember these heroes twenty years from now?
10. Does this honor the emotional reason the project exists?
