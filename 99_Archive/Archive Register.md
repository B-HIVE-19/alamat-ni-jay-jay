## Purpose

The Archive Register organizes retired, rejected, unused, superseded, and historical material for *Alamat ni Jay Jay*.

Archived material is preserved for history and learning. It is not active canon unless officially restored through the Decision Log.

## Archive Areas

| Area | Folder | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Old Ideas | `Old Ideas/` | Early concepts, abandoned hooks, rough prompts, and exploratory notes. |
| Rejected Characters | `Rejected Characters/` | Character concepts that are not part of current canon. |
| Unused Monsters | `Unused Monsters/` | Unused creature, enemy, spirit, or boss concepts. |
| Retired Systems | `Retired Systems/` | Superseded mechanics, prototypes, combat rules, UI systems, and technical approaches. |
| Old Art | `Old Art/` | Replaced sketches, unused visual directions, retired mockups, and superseded asset concepts. |
| Old Storylines | `Old Storylines/` | Retired plotlines, alternate chapters, unused arcs, and superseded story structures. |
| History | `History/` | Historical project records and preserved context. |
| Versions | `Versions/` | Versioned exports, old snapshots, and preserved builds or document bundles. |

## Archive Rules

1. Archive material is not active canon.
2. Do not delete old material simply because the project moved on.
3. Do not reuse archived material without reviewing it against the current Alamat Doctrine, Project Charter, and Decision Log.
4. Restoring archived material requires a new decision or documented revision.
5. Rejected material may still teach us what Alamat is not.

## Restoration Test

Before restoring archived material, ask:

- Does this still honor the emotional reason the project exists?
- Does this make the game more memorable, specific, and adventurous?
- Does this conflict with approved canon?
- Would 12-year-old Jeff be excited this returned?

If any answer creates concern, keep it archived.
